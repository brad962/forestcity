## Post 2 — Educational
**Hook:** You can pressure wash a deck and do serious damage — here's what to do instead.

Wood is not concrete. A pressure washer on a wood deck can splinter the boards, raise the grain, and drive moisture into places it shouldn't go. We see the results all the time: homeowners who DIY'd it and now have a deck that looks rough even after staining.

Soft washing is the right method for wood. Low-pressure water plus a cleaning solution that kills the mold, mildew, and algae sitting in the grain — without wrecking the surface underneath. The wood comes clean and stays clean longer because the biology is actually dead, not just knocked off.

If you've got a deck or fence that needs cleaning before a stain or seal job, don't let someone blast it with full pressure. Ask us about soft washing first. DM us or comment below with questions.

## Post 2 — Tuesday, May 20 (Educational — Roof Algae)

If your roof has black streaks, it's not dirt. It's alive.

Gloeocapsa magma — the algae that grows on asphalt shingles in humid climates like Northeast Ohio. It feeds on the limestone filler in your shingles and spreads every year if left alone.

Pressure washing a roof tears off granules and shortens the life. The right move is soft washing — low pressure, professional solution, kills it at the root.

We've cleaned hundreds of roofs in Cuyahoga, Lake, and Lorain counties. We know what we're doing and we guarantee we won't damage your shingles.

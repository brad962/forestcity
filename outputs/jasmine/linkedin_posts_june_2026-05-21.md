## Post 6 — June 16

A property manager asked me last week: "Is there actually a difference between soft washing and pressure washing?"

Yes — and it matters for your liability exposure.

Pressure washing on siding or roofs can:
- Drive water behind panels (rot risk)
- Strip granules from shingles (voids manufacturer warranty)
- Leave streaks and damage on older surfaces

Soft washing uses low pressure + biodegradable cleaning solution. It kills the algae and mold at the root — instead of just blasting it around. The result lasts longer and doesn't damage the surface.

We only soft wash siding and roofs. If a vendor is pressure washing your roofs, ask questions.

For NE Ohio commercial and residential properties — DM me if you want a walkthrough of the difference for your specific property type.

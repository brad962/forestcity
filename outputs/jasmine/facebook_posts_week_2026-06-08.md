## Post 2 — Educational (Tuesday June 10)

Soft wash vs. pressure wash: here's the short version so you know what to ask for.

**Pressure washing** = high-pressure water. Great for driveways, concrete, brick — hard surfaces that can take it. Bad idea for your siding or roof. High pressure drives water behind vinyl panels, causes rot, and strips granules off shingles. We've seen roofs lose years of life from one bad pressure wash job.

**Soft washing** = low-pressure water + a biodegradable cleaning solution. This is how you clean siding, roofs, decks, and wood safely. The solution does the work — it kills the algae and mold at the root. Pressure alone just knocks it around and it grows back in 90 days.

In Northeast Ohio, with the humidity we get off the lake, soft wash is the right call for most of your home's exterior. "Didn't know soft wash was even a thing" is something we hear from customers every single week.

Now you know. If you have questions about what method is right for your project, drop a comment or DM us — happy to explain.

## Seasonal Language Patterns (NE Ohio Specific)

### Spring (March–May) — Peak booking season
- "The lake humidity really does a number on siding"
- "After the winter, everything needs a good wash"
- "Before the Memorial Day cookout"
- "Getting the house ready for spring"
- "The algae just came right back after the winter"

### Summer (June–August)
- "Before the summer parties"
- "The siding was green by June"
- "Want it done before the kids are out of school"
- "Curb appeal for the summer"

### Fall (September–October) — Second push
- "Before the winter"
- "Don't want another year of buildup"
- "One last clean before the snow"

### Winter / Off-Season
- "Salt stains from the road trucks"
- "The driveway looks terrible from all the winter salt"
- "Booking early for spring"

## High-Converting Subject Lines (from category research)

These patterns convert in NE Ohio home services email:
- Question + local specificity: "Why does every Cleveland driveway look like this in spring?"
- Before/after curiosity gap: "What 2 hours looks like on a Strongsville house"
- Social proof + location: "32 Summit County homeowners booked this week"
- Fear + solution: "Black streaks on your roof? This is what they actually are."
- Urgency + season: "Memorial Day weekend special — book before Thursday"

## NE Ohio Market Specifics (for copy localization)

- **Lake humidity:** Lake Erie moisture → heavy algae/mold growth faster than inland markets. Use this as a local authority signal.
- **Road salt:** Ohio DOT and county trucks salt roads heavily → major driveway and lower-siding staining every winter. This is a real annual problem, not a generic one.
- **Subdivision culture:** Most of Forest City's service area is suburban subdivisions (Strongsville, Avon, Brunswick, Stow) where HOAs and neighbor visibility matter.
- **Harsh winters then hot summers:** Freeze-thaw cycles loosen mold and algae spores; first warm week of spring = instant algae bloom. Booking before it "turns green" is a real motivator.

### 3. Clean Pro Exterior Washing
**Status: Reputation Liability Active | Threat Level: High (but beatable)**

| Factor | June 15 Assessment |
|--------|--------------------|
| Pricing trust | STILL the biggest liability — bait-and-switch pattern persists |
| No-show reputation | Rescheduling complaints likely worsening as peak volume spikes |
| Google profile | 4.0–4.3 range being dragged by recent pricing and no-show reviews |
| Website | More polished than competitors — but agency language erodes trust |
| Peak season behavior | High volume + bad systems = more complaints = more public reviews |

**What's probably happening right now:**
Clean Pro is the market's most dangerous trap for customers. They get the click because they have the best-looking website, then the review pattern kicks in. At peak season, their operational weaknesses get amplified — more jobs booked means more jobs rescheduled, more invoices that don't match quotes.

**This is still Forest City's richest competitive attack target.**

Every week Clean Pro stays busy is another week of fresh bad reviews appearing on their Google profile. The gap between their polished presentation and their delivered experience is the opening.

**Forest City's sharpest attack — June 15 window:**

1. **"Flat-rate quotes. What we say is what you pay."**
   Put this everywhere. Google Ads headline. Facebook ad copy. Estimate confirmation text. Website homepage. This is a direct, factual contrast against Clean Pro's documented pricing pattern — and it works on undecided customers who've already seen Clean Pro's reviews.

2. **"We show up when we say we will — and we confirm by text the day before."**
   Directly attacks their rescheduling reputation. Sounds like we know exactly what customers complain about (because we do).

3. **"We leave the job cleaner than we found it."**
   Directly attacks their cleanup complaint pattern.

**Tactical note for Rick:**
Test "clean pro power washing cleveland" as a competitor keyword in Google Ads. Anyone searching their name + location is considering them. Forest City showing up in that search with "Flat-rate quotes — guaranteed price, no surprises" is a conversion opportunity.

**Watch for:**
- Any reputation management response pattern from Clean Pro — if they start flooding Google with 5-star reviews or responding aggressively to negatives, they know their reputation is bleeding
- Website copy changes — if they suddenly add "guaranteed pricing" language, they've read our playbook

Run a Facebook post or ad targeted to those ZIP codes: "Black streaks on your roof? Most pressure washing companies in the area won't touch it — here's why soft washing is the only safe method." - When property managers in Lorain County call Shine Right for a commercial job and get a no-go, they Google for alternatives — Forest City should be there

## Northeast Ohio Seasonal Patterns — What's Different Here

### The Lake Erie Moisture Problem
NE Ohio properties deal with heavier algae and mold growth than inland markets due to humidity off Lake Erie. This means:
- **Roof soft wash** is more urgently needed here than in drier markets — black staining accelerates
- **Deck and fence cleaning** cycles faster — wood absorbs more moisture, grime builds faster
- This is a VOC win: customers here feel MORE urgency than national ads suggest they should

### Road Salt — The Winter Residue Window
Spring is the peak entry window because:
- Winter road salt deposits accumulate on driveways, foundations, lower siding
- Homeowners see it when the snow melts — desire is high, booking intent peaks March–May
- By June, urgency drops slightly for driveways but picks up for full house wash / curb appeal
- Commercial PMs feel it in their parking areas and building exteriors all spring

### The "Before Guest Season" Trigger
NE Ohio has strong seasonality around:
- Memorial Day weekend (TODAY — peak booking urgency this week)
- July 4th (secondary spike)
- Late September (football season, the "before fall" window)

These are the moments where decision-makers pull the trigger. Touch 3 fires right before Memorial Day by design.

## Competitor Weaknesses — Where to Attack

### 1. Reply Speed
Most local competitors take 2–5 days to reply to quote requests. This is documented in reviews.
**VOC evidence:** "Finally got a response after 3 days — I almost just went with someone else."
**Forest City play:** Same-day quote response as a differentiator. Danny flags it in outreach.

### 2. Commercial Property Neglect
Solo operators don't pitch PMs. They wait for inbound.
**Gap:** Nobody is running a systematic outreach to HOA managers and facility directors.
**Forest City play:** Danny owns this lane. It's essentially uncontested outbound in this market.

### 3. Generic Messaging
Competitors use phrases like "professional pressure washing services" and "quality results guaranteed."
**VOC evidence:** This language does not move people. It's invisible.
**Forest City play:** Use VOC language — "the siding looked green and slimy," "you could see the algae from the street," "we didn't know it could look that white again."

### 4. No Referral Program
Zero local competitors have a structured referral program with contractors.
**Gap:** Carla's lane is completely uncontested.
**Forest City play:** $50/referral + reciprocal referrals. No paperwork. Contractors close quickly.

### 5. No Before/After Content
Local competitors have weak social presence. Most haven't posted in months.
**Gap:** Jasmine's content will stand out immediately in NE Ohio's local feeds.
**Forest City play:** Facebook before/after carousel gets 3–5x more organic reach than text posts in home services.

## NE Ohio Market Dynamics — May Peak

**Why May–June is the real window (not July–August):**
1. Lake Erie humidity means algae blooms early — May 1 is visible on north-facing siding by Willoughby/Mentor
2. Homeowners in NE Ohio are highly price-sensitive to "early season" framing — they'll book faster if they think slots are filling
3. Property managers' fiscal year often ends June 30 — they have budget to spend before July 1 rollover
4. Contractors are at their busiest in May (landscaping startup, siding installs post-winter) — this is when they need referral partnerships locked in, not September

**Lake Effect specific intel:**
- East side suburbs (Willoughby, Mentor, Painesville, Euclid, Cleveland Heights) have the worst algae staining because they're closest to the lake — highest urgency messaging, fastest conversion
- West side (Avon, North Ridgeville, Westlake, Strongsville) is newer construction — less algae, but more HOA-managed communities with exterior standards enforced
- Akron/Summit County: driveways are a bigger deal than siding — road salt damage is more visible on driveways and concrete from the I-77/I-76 corridor

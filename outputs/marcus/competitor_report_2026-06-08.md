### 3. Clean Pro Exterior Washing
**Positioning:** Presents as the "most professional" option. More polished branding than the others. Attempts premium positioning.

| Factor | Intel |
|--------|-------|
| Service area | Greater Cleveland — broader coverage claims |
| Primary services | Full exterior — house, roof, driveway, commercial |
| Roof soft wash | Actively marketed — may be eating our roof cleaning leads |
| Pricing signals | Quotes low, invoices higher — pattern documented in reviews |
| Google profile | 75–150 reviews estimated, 4.0–4.3 range (drags from pricing complaints) |
| Website quality | Better-designed — closest to a real brand in the market |
| Positioning | "Comprehensive exterior solutions" — agency language, not local voice |

**Known weaknesses (HIGH VALUE — exploit these):**
- **Bait-and-switch pricing complaints are documented.** Customers say: "Quote was $200, invoice was $340." This is their biggest liability.
- **Rescheduling and no-show complaints** — job gets moved multiple times without notice
- **Cleanup complaints** — "left debris in the yard" and "didn't rinse the driveway after" appear in reviews
- **Agency language kills trust** — "comprehensive exterior solutions" = no one believes it

**Forest City openings (priority):**
- "Flat-rate quotes — what we say is what you pay" is a direct kill shot at Clean Pro's pricing trust problem. Use this in every ad, every follow-up, every estimate confirmation.
- "We show up when we say we will" — directly attacks their rescheduling weakness
- "We leave the job cleaner than we found it" — directly attacks their cleanup complaint pattern
- **This is Forest City's richest competitive attack target right now.** Clean Pro's reputation problems are live and public — every customer who Googles "Clean Pro power washing Cleveland" and sees bad reviews is a warm inbound lead for us.

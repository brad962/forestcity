## VOC Themes by Category

### Theme 1: The Transformation Reveal
Customers are consistently shocked by the before/after difference. They underestimate how dirty their house got.

**Key phrases:**
- "didn't realize how dirty it was"
- "looks like a different house"
- "like we just moved in"
- "never looked this clean"
- "they were done in two hours — I couldn't believe it"

**Marketing implication:** Lead with the reveal. Before/after content is king. The surprise is the message.

---

### Theme 2: Reliability = The #1 Differentiator
In a market full of "guys on Facebook" who don't show up, reliability is the single most praised attribute in reviews.

**Key phrases:**
- "showed up exactly when they said"
- "did exactly what he said he would do"
- "super responsive"
- "great communicators"

**Marketing implication:** Make reliability a centerpiece, not an afterthought. "We show up when we say" should be a headline, not a bullet point.

---

### Theme 3: Fear of Damage
Customers are afraid the service will damage their home, especially siding, roof, and wood surfaces.

**Key phrases:**
- "worried about damage to the siding"
- "didn't know if it was safe for my roof"
- "afraid of pressure on the wood"
- "thought we were going to have to rip out the deck"

**Marketing implication:** The soft wash explanation is an objection handler AND a trust builder. Address this proactively — before customers ask.

---

### Theme 4: Price Justification
Once the job is done, customers consistently say it was worth the price. But they need permission to spend before booking.

**Key phrases:**
- "worth every penny"
- "very reasonable quote"
- "competitive pricing"
- "don't know why I waited so long"

**Marketing implication:** The price hesitation is a BEFORE problem. The satisfaction is an AFTER feeling. Bridge this gap with packages (entry price point) and outcome language ("what it looks like after" before they buy).

---

### Theme 5: Trigger Events
Customers don't book randomly. There's always a reason they finally called.

| Trigger | Estimated Frequency | Seasonal Peak | Notes |
|---------|--------------------|----|-------|
| About to sell the house | High | April–June, Sept | Pre-listing is a major entry point |
| Neighbor had it done | High | May–Aug | Neighborhood social proof |
| Memorial Day / summer hosting | High | **May peak right now** | Pre-party urgency |
| Finally noticed the algae/streaks | High | April–June | Visibility threshold hits in spring |
| Post-winter / spring cleaning mode | High | March–May | **Currently peak** |
| HOA notice or complaint | Medium | May–June | HOA inspection season |
| Refinance / appraisal | Medium | Spring/Fall | Emerging trigger worth targeting |
| Hosting an event / party | Medium | May–Aug | Curb appeal urgency |
| "Haven't done it in years" | Medium | Ongoing | Overdue trigger |

**Marketing implication:** We're in the heart of the top two triggers RIGHT NOW — spring cleaning mode + Memorial Day prep. Every ad this week should speak to one of those.

---

### Theme 6: The Education Gap
A large portion of customers don't know the difference between soft wash and pressure washing before they book.

**Key phrases:**
- "didn't know soft wash was even a thing"
- "thought they'd just spray it with a hose"
- "didn't realize there was a wrong way to do a roof"
- "pressure washing the roof voided my warranty"
- "tried scrubbing it myself and honestly made it worse"

**Marketing implication:** Education = pre-selling. Customers who understand soft wash before they call are better leads. "Tried scrubbing it myself" is a perfect retargeting hook — they've already tried DIY and failed.

---

### Theme 7 (NEW): Ohio Winter Recovery — Salt and Ice Damage
This is a Northeast Ohio-specific theme with no off-season. Road salt from Cuyahoga, Lake, and Lorain County snowplows hammers driveways and lower siding every year.

**Key phrases:**
- "road salt from the plows stained the whole bottom of the house"
- "winter really did a number on our driveway"
- "after the ice storm the whole front looked gray and beat up"
- "the salt just ate into the concrete"

**Marketing implication:** Spring = Ohio winter recovery season. Frame the spring clean as maintenance, not luxury. "Ohio winters are hard on exteriors — this is the fix" is authentic, true, and regionally specific. No competitor in Cleveland owns this framing yet. Forest City should.

---

### Theme 8 (NEW): The DIY Failure Loop
A growing pattern: customers who tried to do it themselves, failed, and then called a pro. These are warm leads — they've already admitted they can't handle it.

**Key phrases:**
- "spent a whole Saturday with a garden hose and a scrub brush — didn't do anything"
- "tried scrubbing it myself and honestly made it worse"
- "rented a pressure washer and stripped some paint"

**Marketing implication:** Facebook retargeting should include a "tried it yourself?" hook. These people are ready to pay. They just need permission to stop trying.

## What Makes Customers Brag (Post-Job)

These are the things customers tell their neighbors:
1. How different the house looks (the visual transformation)
2. How fast the crew worked
3. That the price was fair / worth it
4. That the company actually showed up and was professional
5. That the roof looks 10 years younger

**Marketing implication:** These are the social proof moments. When asking for reviews, prompt customers with: "What surprised you most?" and "What would you tell a neighbor?"

## Seasonal Language Map — June 2026 (Where We Are Now)

### What customers say in June specifically:
- "Before the Fourth of July" — the big June deadline
- "Father's Day cookout" — immediate window (June 8–14)
- "The kids are out of school now and I finally have time to deal with it"
- "We're having the whole family over this summer"
- "I've been putting it off since April and now it's green"
- "The algae came back — it's like clockwork up here by the lake"

### What NOT to say in June (spring language is stale by now):
- "Spring cleaning" — seasonally off, missed it
- "Post-winter wash" — too early in the season, sounds reactive
- Replace with: "Summer curb appeal," "before the summer parties," "keep it clean all season"

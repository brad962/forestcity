A couple of things worth flagging from the spring section, since most people are working through it right now:

**Roof:** If you see dark streaking (looks black or brown, runs vertically), that's bacteria — not dirt. It's called gloeocapsa magma and it's eating the limestone in your shingles. Soft washing takes it out. Pressure washing makes it worse (and voids most warranties).

**Siding:** Green tint on the north side is almost always algae. It won't go away on its own. One soft wash and it's gone.

One item I want to call out that I've seen come up a lot lately:

**The north-facing wall of your house.**

Most people check the front and sides. The back sometimes. But the north face — the one that gets the least sun and the most moisture — is where algae and mold build up fastest.

In Northeast Ohio, with the lake humidity, that wall can be visibly green in under a year if conditions are right.

It's not a catastrophe. It's just something most people don't catch until it's been there for 2-3 seasons. At that point it takes more solution to kill and longer to fully rinse clean.

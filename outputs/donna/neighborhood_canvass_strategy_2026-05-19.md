## The Concept

Every job you do is a walking billboard. After you finish a house, **5-8 of the neighbors will have seen your truck, your equipment, and the before/after result.** Their house has the same algae yours just cleaned. They're warm — not cold.

This strategy turns every completed job into 2-3 booked follow-up jobs in the same neighborhood.

Cost: $0.
Time: 10 minutes per job.
Expected yield: 1 booking per 3-4 door knocks on average for a service visible from the street.

## The Door Knock Script

*After you finish a job, walk to the 3-4 nearest neighbors. Don't overthink it.*

**Opening:**
> "Hey, sorry to bother you — I just finished up at [house down the street / next door]. I noticed your [siding / driveway / roof] has some of the same staining. We're in the area this week — would you want a quick quote while we're nearby?"

**If they say "maybe" or "how much":**
> "Depends on the size but most houses in this neighborhood run [price range]. Want me to walk around real quick and give you a number?"

**If they say "not right now":**
> "No worries — here's my card. We're out here most weekdays. Just shoot me a text if you change your mind."

**If they say "yes":**
> "Perfect. I'll measure up real quick — takes about 5 minutes. Cash or card both work."

*You don't need a slick pitch. You just knocked. That's 80% of the job.*

---

## Timing

**Best time to knock:** Same day you finish the job, while you're still in the neighborhood and they may have watched you work.

**Second best:** The next morning, 9-11am on a weekday or Saturday morning.

**Don't knock:** After 7pm, during obvious mealtimes, or when cars are gone (no one home).

## Neighborhood Tiers (where this works best)

| Neighborhood Type | Why It Works |
|---|---|
| Suburban streets (1970s-90s homes) | High density of same-age homes with same staining pattern |
| HOA communities | One clean house makes 5 others look bad by comparison |
| Pre-listing neighborhoods | Agents and sellers talk — one "before listing" job ripples |
| Higher-income areas | Easier yes, bigger jobs, more repeat customers |

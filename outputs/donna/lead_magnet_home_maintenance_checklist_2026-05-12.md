# The Northeast Ohio Homeowner's Exterior Maintenance Checklist

*12 months. 7 counties. Everything you need to protect your home from the outside in.*

---

### Why This Matters Here

Northeast Ohio is harder on home exteriors than almost anywhere in the country. You've got:
- **Lake Erie humidity** — feeds algae, mold, and mildew growth on siding and roofs
- **Freeze-thaw cycles** — Ohio gets 20–30+ in a normal winter, expanding and contracting your exterior surfaces
- **Road salt** — stains driveways, walkways, and lower siding every winter
- **Heavy snow loads** — stress on gutters and decks
- **Summer heat + humidity** — deck wood dries and cracks without maintenance

This checklist helps you stay ahead of it. Most items cost nothing. A few need a professional. All of them will save you money compared to ignoring it.

---

## ✅ SPRING CHECKLIST (March–May)

**Walk the full perimeter. Do this after the last snow but before summer heat sets in.**

### Exterior Siding
- [ ] Look for green tint or dark streaking — this is algae/mold, not dirt
- [ ] Check north-facing walls first — they stay moist longest
- [ ] Inspect for any loose or damaged panels that may have shifted over winter
- [ ] Schedule exterior wash if last cleaning was 18+ months ago

### Roof
- [ ] Use binoculars or drone from ground — do NOT walk asphalt shingles
- [ ] Look for dark streaking (gloeocapsa magma bacteria — eats shingle limestone)
- [ ] Look for missing, curling, or blistering shingles
- [ ] Check flashing around chimney and skylights for rust/separation
- [ ] Clear any debris from valleys (leaves, branches)
- [ ] Schedule soft wash if streaking is visible — **never pressure wash a roof**

### Gutters & Downspouts
- [ ] Clear all debris from gutters (spring leaves/seeds are the worst clogger)
- [ ] Check downspout discharge is 3+ feet from foundation
- [ ] Look for sagging or separated seams
- [ ] Flush with hose to confirm free flow

### Driveway & Walkways
- [ ] Inspect for winter salt damage and staining
- [ ] Look for new cracking from freeze-thaw
- [ ] Seal any cracks before they expand (late spring is ideal timing)
- [ ] Schedule pressure wash cleaning before summer entertaining

### Deck & Fence
- [ ] Check all boards for rot, splitting, and raised nails (safety issue)
- [ ] Look for gray, weathered color — this means the wood is drying out
- [ ] Check posts and footings for stability
- [ ] Plan wash + stain/seal before summer (do wash first, stain after it dries)

### Foundation
- [ ] Check grading slopes away from house
- [ ] Clear any mulch/debris piled against the foundation
- [ ] Inspect basement/crawl space walls for moisture after first rain

## ✅ SUMMER CHECKLIST (June–August)

### Mid-Season Check (July)
- [ ] Inspect siding for new algae growth (humid summer can accelerate it)
- [ ] Check for wasp/bee nests forming under eaves
- [ ] Clean out gutters after spring storms (cottonwood and seed pods clog fast)
- [ ] Inspect deck boards for additional splitting from heat

### Ongoing
- [ ] Keep 18" clearance between soil/mulch and wood siding
- [ ] Trim back trees and shrubs touching house (holds moisture against siding)
- [ ] Clean outdoor AC unit — clear all debris from condenser fins

---

## ✅ FALL CHECKLIST (September–November)

### Pre-Winter Prep — Do This Before First Freeze

**Exterior Wash (October)**
- [ ] Schedule pre-winter exterior cleaning (removes algae + mold before it freezes in over winter)
- [ ] Especially important for north-facing siding and roof
- [ ] Clean the deck before closing it for winter — moisture under debris causes rot

**Gutters**
- [ ] Full gutter cleanout after leaf fall (late October/early November)
- [ ] Install gutter guards if you're doing this every year
- [ ] Check for ice dam risk — poor insulation shows up as icicle formation

**Driveway**
- [ ] Apply concrete sealer before first freeze (last chance until spring)
- [ ] Fill any cracks opened up from summer heat

**Windows & Doors**
- [ ] Check weatherstripping — replace if daylight visible around frame
- [ ] Re-caulk any gaps in window and door trim
- [ ] Check storm windows and doors are latched and sealed

**Roof**
- [ ] Final visual inspection before snow covers it
- [ ] Clear any soft spots (leaves packed against flashing)

---

## ✅ WINTER CHECKLIST (December–February)

- [ ] After heavy snow: check for ice dams at eave lines
- [ ] Clear snow from low-slope roof sections with a roof rake (from ground)
- [ ] Monitor gutters for ice buildup / icicle formation
- [ ] Inspect basement walls after prolonged thaw
- [ ] Book spring cleaning now — good contractors fill up by February/March

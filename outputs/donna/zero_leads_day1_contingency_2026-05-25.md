# Zero Leads — Day 1 Contingency Plan
### Donna Park | Growth & Launch Strategist | 2026-05-25

> **This file exists so you don't panic.** Facebook and Google campaigns need 24–72 hours to exit the learning phase and start delivering efficiently. Zero leads on Day 1 is normal. This guide tells you exactly what it means, what to check, and what to do — so you make smart decisions instead of reactive ones.

---

## First: What "Zero Leads" on Day 1 Actually Means

| Scenario | What It Means | What To Do |
|----------|--------------|------------|
| Campaigns are active, spending money, 0 leads by 5pm Day 1 | Learning phase — totally normal | Check the 3-point diagnostic below, then wait |
| Campaigns are active, spending money, 0 leads by Day 3 | May need creative or targeting adjustment | See Day 3 protocol in `outputs/rick/facebook_ads_first_week_monitoring_guide_2026-05-23.md` |
| Campaigns showing as "Active" but $0 spend by noon | Something is wrong (billing, ad set issue) | See Emergency Checklist below |
| Campaigns showing as "In Review" | Normal — Facebook reviews all new ads (24–48h) | Wait. Check your email for approval notification. |
| Campaigns showing as "Disapproved" | Ad policy issue | Check the reason in Ads Manager → fix the flagged item |

**The biggest mistake first-time advertisers make:** Changing ad settings within the first 48 hours. Every change resets the learning phase. If you touch the budget, targeting, or creative before Day 3, you are paying to restart the algorithm from zero.

**The rule: DO NOT EDIT ANYTHING before Day 3, even if you have 0 leads.**

## 3-Point Day 1 Diagnostic (5 minutes, do at 5pm)

Run through these 3 checks before deciding anything is wrong:

### Check 1: Are the campaigns actually spending?
Go to Ads Manager → Campaigns tab
- If you see a **daily spend number** (even $5) — campaigns are running. ✅
- If you see **$0 spend** by noon — something is wrong. See Emergency Checklist.

### Check 2: Are you in the learning phase?
Campaigns tab → look for the "Learning" label under your campaign name.
- If "Learning" → normal. The algorithm is gathering data. ✅
- If "Learning Limited" → issue with small audience or low budget. Usually resolves in 2-3 days.

### Check 3: Is the Lead Form working?
Go to: Facebook Page → Publishing Tools → Lead Ad Forms → Preview your form
- Submit a test entry yourself (use a fake email like test@test.com)
- Did you receive a notification? If yes → form is working. ✅
- If no → check notification settings per `outputs/rick/ad_lead_notification_setup_2026-05-25.md`

## If All 3 Checks Pass and You Still Have 0 Leads

This is the most common Day 1 scenario. Here's what's actually happening:

Facebook campaigns typically generate leads starting on Day 2-3 after the algorithm has run enough impressions to find the right audience. 

**Benchmark expectations by time:**
| Time Point | Expected Leads (FB $30/day) | Expected Leads (Google $20/day) |
|------------|----------------------------|----------------------------------|
| End of Day 1 | 0-2 (maybe 0 is fine) | 0-3 calls (maybe 0 is fine) |
| End of Day 3 | 1-5 total | 2-8 calls total |
| End of Day 7 | 5-15 total | 5-20 calls total |

If you hit Day 3 with 0 leads total across both platforms, THEN consult the monitoring guides.

## Emergency Checklist (If Campaigns Show $0 Spend by Noon Day 1)

1. **Check billing:** Ads Manager → Billing → is a payment method on file? Any failed payment?
2. **Check campaign status:** Is the toggle ON (green)? Sometimes campaigns are toggled off accidentally.
3. **Check ad schedule:** Did you set a schedule that excluded today's date or hours?
4. **Check ad approval status:** Any ads showing "Disapproved" or "In Review"?
5. **Check audience size:** Is the estimated audience size showing "Too small" or under 1,000?

If billing fails → add a new payment method. Campaigns resume automatically within 1 hour.
If ads disapproved → read the reason, fix the flagged element (usually the hook copy), resubmit.

## The Psychology Note

Zero leads on Day 1 will feel bad. You spent money, you did all the setup, and nothing happened yet.

This is how every single paid ads campaign starts. The algorithm needs data to learn who clicks your ad and submits a form. It doesn't have that data on Day 1 — it's collecting it. 

The money you spent on Day 1 isn't wasted. It's buying the algorithm the data it needs to send you profitable leads on Day 3, 5, and beyond.

The decision tree:
- **Day 1, 0 leads:** Check the 3-point diagnostic. If all pass, stay the course.
- **Day 3, 0 leads:** Read the monitoring guides. Maybe adjust targeting, not copy.
- **Day 7, 0 leads:** Talk to Rick. Something needs to change.

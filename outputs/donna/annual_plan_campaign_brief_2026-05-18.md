### Donna Park | Growth & Launch Strategist | 2026-05-18
*Start building now (May–June). Execute in August–September.*

---

## The Opportunity

Every customer Forest City cleans this May–June is a warm lead for a recurring Annual Plan — two visits per year (spring + pre-winter), automatic booking, and predictable revenue for the business.

**The math:** If 15% of 2026 peak-season customers convert to Annual Plan:
- Even 10 customers × $400 average annual plan value = $4,000 guaranteed recurring revenue
- Year 2 renewal rate for annual maintenance customers is typically 70–80% in local services
- This is the beginning of the subscription revenue layer

**The window:** Lock them in while the result is still fresh. The best time to ask for a recurring commitment is right after they see the transformation. Don't wait until October.

---

## Campaign Name: The Annual Plan Push — Summer 2026

**Timeline:** Seed in June, primary push in August, close by September 30.

**Target audience:** Anyone who had a job completed in May, June, or July 2026.

**Goal:** Convert 15% of completed 2026 jobs to Annual Plan customers.

---

## The Offer (Define Before Launching)

**Recommended structure:**
- 2-visit Annual Plan: Spring wash + pre-winter wash (October/November)
- Price: Full price for spring visit + 15% discount on fall visit
- Bonus: Priority scheduling — booked before general public each season
- Payment: Pay for spring at booking; fall visit invoiced in October

**Positioning:** Frame it as "lock in your spot" + "we handle it automatically" — not just a discount.

> "We book you in every spring and every fall. You never have to remember to call. Your house stays clean, your curb appeal stays up, and you save money on the second visit."

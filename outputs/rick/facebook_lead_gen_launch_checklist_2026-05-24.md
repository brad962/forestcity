## Why This Checklist Exists

Most first-time Facebook ad launchers click "Publish" and then wait 24 hours to find out the Pixel wasn't firing, leads weren't going anywhere, or the learning phase got disrupted on Day 2. These 8 checks take 20 minutes and prevent the most expensive mistakes.

---

## Checklist — Do These in Order

### Block 1: Pixel Setup (5 minutes)
**Before creating the campaign:**

- [ ] **Install Facebook Pixel on your website** (if not already done)
  - Go to: Events Manager → Pixels → Add New Data Source → Copy the base code → paste into website header
  - If you don't have a website or it's not pixel-ready, use **Lead Gen Forms only** (no website needed)
  - **Lead Gen Forms are actually BETTER for Forest City** — the lead never leaves Facebook, conversion rate is higher

- [ ] **Confirm Pixel is firing** (if you have a website)
  - Install "Meta Pixel Helper" Chrome extension → visit your site → green checkmark = working
  - If red: paste the pixel code into the `<head>` tag, not the footer

### Block 2: Lead Notification Setup (5 minutes)
**CRITICAL — This is the #1 thing people forget.**

Without this, leads sit in Facebook silently and Bradley never sees them until he logs into Ads Manager.

- [ ] **Turn on lead email notifications:**
  - Go to Meta Business Suite → Inbox → Leads → Notification Settings
  - Enable: "Email me when I receive a new lead"
  - Enter: your real email address (the one Bradley checks)

- [ ] **Set up CRM integration or manual download:**
  - Easiest: Download leads manually from Ads Manager → Campaigns → View Charts → Download Leads (daily check)
  - Better: Connect Zapier → Facebook Lead Ads → send new lead to Google Sheets or email

- [ ] **Test: Submit a test lead**
  - After creating the Lead Gen Form in the ad, use Facebook's "Preview" to submit a test lead
  - Confirm you receive the email notification within 5 minutes
  - If you don't receive the test lead: notification isn't set up correctly

### Block 3: Lead Gen Form Settings (5 minutes)
**Required fields for Forest City leads:**

- [ ] Form type: **More Volume** (not Higher Intent — lower friction = more leads for services)
- [ ] Required fields: First Name, Last Name, Phone Number (NOT just email)
  - Phone = required. You need to call or text these people. Email-only leads convert at 15%. Phone leads convert at 45%.
- [ ] Add 1 qualifying question: "What's your zip code?" — filters out-of-area leads
- [ ] Thank-you screen message: "We'll text you within the hour to schedule your free quote."
- [ ] Link to privacy policy (Facebook requires this — use a simple one-liner on your site or Google Doc)

### Block 4: Budget and Targeting Confirmation (3 minutes)

- [ ] **Starting budget:** $15/day (NOT $30/day) for the first 3 days
  - Facebook needs ~50 conversions to exit the learning phase
  - $15/day at $8–$12 cost per lead = 3–5 leads per day
  - Scale to $30/day after 50 total leads and a cost per lead you can live with
  
- [ ] **Audience:** Homeowners 30–65 in Cuyahoga, Lake, Lorain, Summit, Medina, Geauga, Portage counties
  - Estimated audience size: 300,000–600,000 (Facebook will tell you when targeting is set)
  - If audience is < 100,000: too narrow, widen the radius or age range
  - If audience is > 1,000,000: add more interests to tighten it

- [ ] **Creative confirmed:** Use before/after images (NOT stock photos). See `outputs/jasmine/` for existing content.

### Block 5: Lead Response SOP Confirmed (2 minutes)

- [ ] **Read before first lead arrives:** `outputs/rick/google_guaranteed_lead_response_sop_2026-05-23.md`
  - The same response timing principle applies to Facebook leads: respond within 5–15 minutes
  - Facebook tracks your response time and rewards fast responders with cheaper leads
  - Script: "Hey [Name], it's Bradley from Forest City Power Washing — just saw your request for a quote. Are you free for a quick call today?"

## The 5 Things That Kill the Learning Phase

1. **Changing the budget more than 20% in the first week** — Facebook restarts learning every time
2. **Pausing the campaign in the first 3 days** — resets the algorithm
3. **Editing the ad creative** — forces restart
4. **Too narrow an audience** (< 100,000) — algorithm can't find enough people to learn from
5. **0 leads in first 3 days** — either targeting is too narrow OR the ad isn't stopping the scroll. If zero leads by Day 3: change the image, not the targeting.

## What Good Looks Like (Week 1 Benchmarks)

| Metric | Target | Action if Below |
|--------|--------|-----------------|
| Cost Per Lead | $8–$18 | > $20: change image/hook |
| CTR (click-through rate) | > 1.5% | < 1%: test new headline |
| Form Completion Rate | > 25% | < 15%: simplify form fields |
| Leads by Day 3 | 3–10 | 0: change creative immediately |

## After the First Lead Comes In

1. **Call within 15 minutes.** "Hey [Name], Bradley from Forest City..."
2. If no answer: text immediately after. "Hey, I saw your quote request — free anytime today to talk through what you need?"
3. If no response in 24 hours: one final follow-up text. "Still happy to get you a free quote this week. Just reply with your address and I'll pull together a number."
4. **Do NOT use email for Facebook leads.** They filled out a form on their phone. Text is the right channel.
5. Log the lead in Workiz immediately — even before the call is confirmed.

# Google Ads — First Week Monitoring Guide
*Rick Santoro | Paid Ads Specialist*
*Written: 2026-05-24 | Use starting Tuesday May 26*

---

## Before You Check Anything: Set Realistic Expectations

Google Ads learning phase = first 7–14 days. The algorithm is testing which searches convert, what time of day works, what devices. **Do not optimize in the first 48 hours.** You will kneecap the machine before it learns.

The goal for Week 1 is NOT leads. The goal is:
- Confirm the ads are actually showing (impressions)
- Confirm clicks are coming in
- Confirm conversion tracking fired at least once
- Catch any obvious setup errors

---

## DAY 1 CHECK (Tuesday May 26 evening or Wednesday May 27 morning)

### What to Look At
Log into Google Ads → All Campaigns → Last 1 day

**✅ Impressions > 0**
If you see zero impressions after 4 hours, the ad is not showing. Check:
- Campaign status = Active (not Paused)
- Billing confirmed (Google holds first charges 24h)
- Location targeting not too narrow
- Keyword match types not too restrictive

**✅ Clicks > 0**
If impressions are coming in but 0 clicks, your ad copy may not be compelling or bids are too low to show in a prominent position. Normal to have 0–5 clicks on Day 1.

**✅ CTR Check**
| Keyword Type | Healthy CTR Day 1 |
|---|---|
| Branded ("Forest City Power Washing") | >8% |
| Service + location ("power washing Cleveland") | >3% |
| General ("pressure washing") | >1.5% |

Low CTR on Day 1 is normal. The ad rank improves as Quality Score builds.

**✅ Calls / Conversions**
Check if your phone call extension fired. If you have call tracking: did ANY calls come through? Even 1 is a win Day 1. Google tracks 60+ second calls as conversions.

**What NOT to Do Day 1:**
- Do NOT adjust bids
- Do NOT pause keywords
- Do NOT change ad copy
- Do NOT change targeting

## DAY 3 CHECK (Friday May 29)

By Day 3 you have real data. Now look more carefully.

### Metrics to Pull: Last 3 Days

**Impressions / Clicks**
- Power washing + location keywords: expect 30–150 impressions, 3–15 clicks at $30/day budget
- If clicks are 0 after 3 days: bid is too low — raise max CPC by 50%

**CPC (Cost Per Click) Benchmarks — NE Ohio Power Washing**
| Keyword Category | Expected CPC |
|---|---|
| "power washing [city]" | $2.50–$5.00 |
| "house washing northeast ohio" | $2.00–$4.50 |
| "soft wash roof cleaning" | $3.00–$6.00 |
| "pressure washing [county]" | $1.50–$3.50 |

If CPC is $8+: Your Quality Score is low. This means ad relevance or landing page needs work. Do NOT just raise budget — fix relevance first.

**Quality Score (view at keyword level)**
Score 1–10. Below 5 = your ad/landing page doesn't match the keyword well.
- Score 7–10 = healthy
- Score 4–6 = room to improve
- Score 1–3 = fix immediately (keyword-to-ad relevance issue)

**Phone Calls**
By Day 3 you should have at least 1 call if targeting is correct. If 0 calls and 10+ clicks: either the landing page (or call extension) has friction, or the clicks are from the wrong people. Check search terms report (see below).

**Search Terms Report — Day 3 Must-Check**
Go to: Keywords → Search Terms
See ACTUAL queries that triggered your ads. Look for:
- ✅ Good: "power washing Cuyahoga County", "house washing Cleveland", "soft wash roof Akron"
- ❌ Bad: "car wash near me", "power washing machine for sale", "DIY pressure washer"

Add bad search terms as NEGATIVE keywords immediately. This is the most important Day 3 action.

**Day 3 Decision Tree:**
```
0 impressions → Check billing + campaign status + bid floor
Impressions but 0 clicks → Raise max CPC 50%, check ad copy
Clicks but 0 calls → Check phone extension, landing page, search terms for irrelevant traffic
Calls coming in → ✅ Do nothing. Watch and wait.
```

## DAY 7 CHECK (following Tuesday, June 2)

By Day 7 you have enough data for real optimization decisions.

### Metrics to Review

**Cost Per Lead (CPL)**
Calculate: Total spend ÷ number of calls/form fills
| CPL | What It Means |
|---|---|
| < $20 | Excellent — scale up budget |
| $20–$40 | Good — standard for home services |
| $40–$70 | Acceptable — optimize Search Terms + Quality Score |
| $70+ | Problem — pause weak keywords, fix relevance |

**Conversion Rate**
Calls ÷ Clicks. Healthy: 5–15% for power washing
Below 3%: landing page issue, wrong audience, or misleading ad copy

**Revenue Math Check (Day 7)**
At $30/day × 7 days = $210 spent.
If you've booked even 1 job from ads = break-even or better.
Typical close rate on Google Ads leads for home services: 40–60%.
So: 5 leads → 2–3 jobs → $700–$1,800 revenue.

### Day 7 Optimization Actions

**Lower performers (CPC > $8 and 0 conversions):**
Pause or reduce bids. Don't delete — just throttle.

**Top performers (calls coming in, CPC < $5):**
Increase bid cap by 20%. Let the winner run.

**Ad schedule:**
Check hour-of-day and day-of-week performance.
Most home service calls happen: Mon–Fri 7am–8pm, Sat 8am–1pm.
Pause overnight hours if burning budget with 0 calls.

**Negative keywords to add by Day 7 (standard for power washing):**
- diy, rental, rent, machine, equipment, supplies, parts
- car wash, auto, vehicle
- jobs, careers, hiring, employment
- free, cheap (if you don't want price-shoppers)

## Common First-Week Mistakes That Kill Results

1. **Editing ads in the first 48 hours.** Resets the learning phase. Don't.
2. **Using only broad match keywords.** You'll match "pressure washing machine rental." Use phrase match ("power washing cleveland") or exact match ([power washing cleveland ohio]).
3. **Not adding negative keywords before launch.** Day 1 setup requirement — not optional.
4. **Setting budget too low to exit learning phase.** Under $20/day may not generate enough click data for Google to optimize. $30/day is the floor.
5. **No conversion tracking.** If Google can't see what converts, it optimizes for clicks not calls. Verify conversion tracking fired at least once before Day 7.
6. **Checking the account every hour.** Check 3x: Day 1 evening, Day 3, Day 7. Not in between.

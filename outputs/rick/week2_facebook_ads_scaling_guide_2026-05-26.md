## Decision Matrix: What Did Week 1 Show?

### ✅ WORKING — CTR > 1.5%, Cost Per Lead < $40, 3+ leads

**You're in learning phase graduation territory. Here's how to scale:**

**1. Budget: Increase by 20% max (do NOT double)**
- Current budget: $X/day → new budget: $X × 1.2/day
- Example: $15/day → $18/day. Never jump to $30/day — resets learning.
- Wait 7 more days before touching budget again.

**2. Add Lookalike Audience (Week 2 unlock)**
- Facebook Ads Manager → Audiences → Create Audience → Lookalike
- Source: Website Custom Audience (people who visited your site)
- Size: 1% (most similar) for NE Ohio
- Why: You now have 7 days of pixel data — use it to find more people like your converters

**3. Test a second creative (NOT instead of — IN ADDITION TO)**
- Keep your best Week 1 ad running unchanged
- Duplicate the ad set → swap the creative to a before/after photo (if you have one by now)
- Let both run for 7 days — Facebook will auto-favor the winner
- Do NOT turn off your control ad until the test wins clearly

**4. Expand geographic targeting slightly**
- Add one adjacent city to your targeting (e.g., add Strongsville if targeting Cleveland)
- Do NOT go statewide — relevance score drops

---

### ⚠️ MIXED — Some leads but CTR low (<1%) or CPL high ($50+)

**Don't scale yet. Optimize first:**

**1. Swap the hook using your VOC library**
- Open `outputs/rick/facebook_ad_creative_voc_refresh_2026-05-24.md`
- Pick the hook with the highest emotional resonance for homeowners
- Duplicate your current ad → change ONLY the headline and primary text
- Run both for 7 days

**2. Check your audience size**
- If audience < 30,000: too narrow — add interests or loosen age range
- If audience > 500,000: too broad — tighten to homeowners 30-65 only

**3. Verify your Facebook Pixel is firing**
- Events Manager → check that "ViewContent" is tracking on your website
- If no data: your form fills aren't being counted as conversions

---

### 🔴 NOT WORKING — 0 leads after 7 days, $0.50+ CPC

**Stop and diagnose before spending more:**

Checklist:
- [ ] Is your Facebook Pixel installed? (Events Manager → Activity)
- [ ] Is your ad actually approved and running? (check "Active" status)
- [ ] Did you get impressions? (>1,000 impressions means Facebook showed it — low CTR is the issue)
- [ ] Is your landing page / lead form loading? (test on mobile)

**Most likely fix:** Creative issue. The hook isn't stopping the scroll. Swap to the before/after photo format with text overlay: "Before vs. After. Which one is your neighbor's house?" — visual pattern interrupt.

## What NOT to Do in Week 2

| Action | Why It Hurts |
|--------|-------------|
| Edit budget up or down more than 20% | Resets learning phase — wastes 5–7 days |
| Turn off your best ad | Loses social proof (likes/comments) on that ad |
| Change targeting while changing creative | Can't tell which variable moved the metric |
| Pause for 24h+ then restart | Algorithm relearns from scratch — worst case |
| Add CBO (Campaign Budget Optimization) | Only do this in Week 3+ with 3+ ad sets |

## Logging This Week's Leads

Fill in `outputs/donna/launch_week_lead_log_2026-05-26.md` daily.
After 14 days, you'll have enough data to calculate your true CPA (cost per acquisition = cost per booked job) and decide if ads are profitable at current spend.

**Revenue math:**
- If your average job is $350 and close rate is 30%:
- CPA target = $350 × 0.30 = $105 max per lead
- If you're getting leads at $40 and booking 1 in 3 = $120 CPA = slightly above target
- Fix: improve response speed (within 5 min = 9x conversion) OR improve close rate

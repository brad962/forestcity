# Facebook Lead Gen — Inbound Lead Response SOP
### Created: 2026-05-24 | Rick Santoro — Paid Ads Specialist
*Use this the moment a Facebook Lead Gen form comes in. Read before Tuesday May 26 launch.*

---

## Why This Matters
Facebook Lead Gen form submissions are warm — they clicked your ad, filled out a form, gave you their name/phone/email. But they submitted to 2–4 other companies at the same time. The first company to call wins 50%+ of the time. Response time is everything.

**Target: call within 5 minutes of submission.**

---

## Step 1 — Set Up Lead Notifications (Do This BEFORE Ads Launch)
*You only set this up once.*

1. Go to **Meta Business Suite** (business.facebook.com)
2. Top-right → **Settings** → **Notifications**
3. Enable: **New lead from Lead Ad** → Email + Push notification
4. Also enable in **Facebook Business Manager → Leads Center** — set email to your phone's email
5. Download the **Facebook Business Suite app** on your phone — push notifications are instant

*Without this, you'll check leads manually and miss the 5-minute window.*

---

## Step 2 — When You Get the Notification

**Within 5 minutes:**

1. Open Business Suite app or **business.facebook.com → Leads Center**
2. Find the new lead — you'll see: Name, Phone, Email, City
3. **Call immediately.** Do not text first.

---

## Step 3 — Live Call Script

**When they answer:**

> "Hi, is this [Name]? This is Bradley calling from Forest City Power Washing — I saw you just requested a quote online. Did I catch you at a good time?"

*[If yes]:*
> "Great. Can you tell me a little about the project — is this a house wash, driveway, roof, or all of the above?"

*Listen. Then:*
> "Okay, based on that I can give you a solid ballpark. What's the address so I can pull it up on Google Maps and get you an accurate number?"

*After getting address:*
> "Give me 30 seconds."
> *(Look at Google Street View or Zillow)*
> "For a home that size in [City], you're looking at roughly $[X]–$[X]. That includes [services mentioned]. I could come take a quick look this week and give you an exact number — would [Day] or [Day] work for you?"

*Close the booking:*
> "Perfect. I'll put you down for [Day] at [Time]. I'll shoot you a text confirmation right now."

---

## Step 4 — If They Don't Answer (Voicemail)

Leave this voicemail:

> "Hi [Name], this is Bradley from Forest City Power Washing — I'm calling about the quote you just requested. I'll get you taken care of, just give me a call back at [your number]. I'll also send you a text so you have my number. Talk soon!"

**Immediately after voicemail — send this text:**

> "Hi [Name], Bradley from Forest City Power Washing. I just left you a voicemail about your quote request. What's the project — house wash, roof, driveway? I can usually get you a number same day. Text me back anytime."

---

## Step 5 — If No Response in 2 Hours

Send follow-up text:

> "Hey [Name] — just following up on your Forest City Power Washing quote request. Are you still looking to get this done before summer? Happy to give you a quick ballpark over text if that's easier."

---

## Step 6 — Day 2 Follow-Up (if still no response)

> "Hi [Name], Bradley from Forest City Power Washing. Wanted to check — are you still interested in a quote? We're booking into June now and spots are filling fast. Just reply YES if you want me to hold a slot."

---

## Step 7 — Log in Workiz

After every lead (answered or not):
- Create a new job in Workiz
- Client: [Name], [Phone], [Email], [Address]
- Job Type: Power Washing
- Status: Quote Requested
- Notes: "Facebook Lead Ad — [date] — [called/left VM/texted]"

This is how you track conversion rate from Facebook leads → booked jobs.

## Common Objections

**"I'm just getting quotes"**
> "Totally — that's smart. Give me 2 minutes and I can at least give you a ballpark so you have something to compare against. What's the address?"

**"I'm not ready yet"**
> "No problem. When are you thinking? I can put a soft hold on the schedule so you get a date that works. We fill up fast in June."

**"I'll think about it"**
> "Completely understand. Is there anything specific that would help you decide? Sometimes I can answer questions over text — no pressure."

**"Too expensive"**
> "What number were you thinking? Sometimes I can work something out depending on timing, or we can start with one area and come back for the rest."

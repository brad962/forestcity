## The Problem

Google Ads has two ways to generate leads:
1. **Form click** → lands on your website → fills out contact form → you get a name + email
2. **Call extension click** → they tap the phone number IN the Google ad → calls your phone directly

**Form fills are tracked automatically** in the launch_week_lead_log — you get their info.

**Direct calls from ads are invisible** unless you know what to look for. If you just see a random call come in from a 440 number and book a job, you'll probably mark it as "organic" when it was actually a $15–$35 Google Ads conversion. This makes your Google Ads look worse than they are and can lead you to pause campaigns that are actually working.

---

## How to Tell a Google Ads Call From an Organic Call

### Method 1: Google Ads Call History (Best)
1. Go to **ads.google.com**
2. Left menu → **Reports** → **Call Details**
3. You'll see a log of every phone call generated by your ads with: date, time, call duration, area code, campaign name
4. Cross-reference with your own call log — if the times match, it was a Google Ads call

**Check this every morning.** Takes 2 minutes.

### Method 2: Note the Number They Called
When Google Ads call extensions are active, Google routes calls through a **forwarding number** — a Google-generated number that forwards to yours. When a call comes in through this forwarding number, your phone will ring as normal but the incoming caller ID on your end shows the Google forwarding number, not the caller's real number.

- The call confirmation in Google Ads will show the original caller's area code
- Your phone receives it normally — book the job, then log it

### Method 3: Ask Them
The simplest fallback: when someone calls and they seem to know you from Google ("I searched power washing Cleveland"), ask:
> "Just curious — were you searching on Google or did someone refer you?"

Most people remember. If they say Google, it's almost certainly a Google Ads call (paid) vs. an organic Google search result click (free). Both count as wins, but ads credit needs to go to the right campaign.

## What to Do When You Get a Google Ads Call

**Within 5 minutes of hanging up:**

1. **Log it in the launch_week_lead_log** (outputs/rick/launch_week_lead_log_2026-05-25.md)
   - Date, Source: **Google Ads call**, Name, Phone, Response Time
   - Mark Quoted Y/N and Booked Y/N after you follow up

2. **Add to pipeline_data.json** — use Nina's ad_lead_tracker guide (outputs/nina/ad_lead_tracker_2026-05-25.md)
   - Set `"lead_source": "Google Ads call"` (NOT "Google Ads form" — this distinction matters for ROI math later)
   - Set stage to `"Contacted"` if you had a live conversation
   - Set stage to `"New Lead"` if you left voicemail

3. **If they booked:** Follow first_ad_booking_protocol (outputs/tommy/first_ad_booking_protocol_2026-05-25.md)
   - This is where you calculate your actual CPA (cost per acquisition) for the first time
   - Before/after photos + annual plan pitch + Google review request all follow from here

## Ad Call Notification Setup (if not done yet)

From ad_lead_notification_setup_2026-05-25.md:
1. In Google Ads: **Tools & Settings** → **Conversions** → set "Call duration" to 30 seconds (counts as a conversion once they've been on the line 30 sec)
2. Turn on **email conversion alerts** — you'll get an email any time a call is counted as a conversion

**NE Ohio power washing Google Ads benchmarks:**
- Good call rate: 2–5% of clicks → calls (most clicks go to website)
- Budget: $20–$30/day Google → expect 1–3 qualified calls/day at scale
- Target CPA (cost per booking): under $150

If by Day 7 you've had 0 calls, check:
- Is call extension showing? (ads.google.com → Extensions → Calls)
- Is the phone number correct in the extension?
- Are campaigns spending? (check daily budget delivery)

## Daily 2-Minute Google Ads Call Check

**Morning routine (8am, after Nina's daily hot leads report):**

1. ads.google.com → Reports → Call Details — any calls since yesterday?
2. Cross-reference with your call log — did you already log them?
3. Update launch_week_lead_log with yesterday's calls

That's it. 2 minutes. This is how you know your $20/day is producing real calls.

# Northeast Ohio Home Exterior Maintenance Checklist
### Your 12-Month Guide to Protecting Your Investment
*From Forest City Power Washing — Cleveland, OH*

---

## Why This Checklist Matters in Northeast Ohio

NE Ohio is hard on houses. Lake Erie humidity feeds algae and mold year-round. Road salt and winter grime get into every crack. Spring thaw heaves concrete and opens gaps in siding. Most homeowners don't notice the damage until it's expensive.

This checklist is built for this climate — not a generic national template. Follow it and your house looks better, lasts longer, and costs you less over time.

---

## March — Start-of-Season Inspection

**Exterior Walk-Around**
- [ ] Walk the full perimeter of your house after snow melts
- [ ] Look at siding: any green or black patches? That's algae/mold from winter moisture
- [ ] Look at your roof: black streaks? That's Gloeocapsa Magma — roof algae. It eats shingles
- [ ] Check gutters: are they still attached? Any sagging from ice weight?
- [ ] Look at concrete (driveway, sidewalk, patio): any new cracks from freeze-thaw cycles?

**What to Do Now**
- [ ] Schedule your spring exterior wash before peak-season slots fill up (April–May books fast)
- [ ] If your roof has black streaks, book a soft wash — pressure washing will void your warranty
- [ ] Clear any debris from gutters and downspout exits

---

## April — Spring Clean Month

**The Big Jobs (book by end of March to get April dates)**
- [ ] **House washing** — removes the full winter's worth of algae, mold, road salt, and grime
- [ ] **Driveway cleaning** — salt and winter grit embed into concrete. A good pressure wash before sealing prevents long-term damage
- [ ] **Deck/patio check** — winter moisture causes wood fibers to open up. Clean before you put furniture out

**Quick Checks**
- [ ] Inspect window caulking and seals — winter can crack them
- [ ] Check door and garage door weather stripping
- [ ] Look for any paint peeling on siding — often a moisture issue underneath

---

## May — Peak Season Prep

**Curb Appeal Month** (cookouts, open houses, and neighbors noticing everything)
- [ ] If you're thinking about selling this year, this is the month to get the house washed — before listing photos
- [ ] Before listing, power wash driveway and walkway — first thing buyers see
- [ ] **Roof check:** UV rays are strongest May–August. Algae on shingles grows fastest in this window. A soft wash now protects your roof through summer
- [ ] Clean outdoor furniture before the season starts (soft brush + mild solution)
- [ ] Check deck boards for splinters, loose nails, or soft spots before regular use

**NE Ohio Specific**
- [ ] If you have a basement, check window wells for debris after spring rains
- [ ] Inspect the ground-level gap between siding and foundation — mold loves this spot

---

## June — Early Summer Check-In

- [ ] Mow and edge so the lawn looks as good as the house
- [ ] Check downspouts: are they diverting water away from your foundation? (6 feet minimum)
- [ ] Look at wood deck/fence: if you power washed in April, is it ready to seal or stain? Wait 48–72 hours after washing before sealing
- [ ] First generation of algae on siding often appears by June in NE Ohio — wipe visible patches before they spread

---

## July — Mid-Summer

- [ ] Inspect roof edges and fascia boards for moisture damage
- [ ] Check if gutters are clean — summer storms bring leaves even before fall
- [ ] If you have a concrete driveway, look for new cracks from summer heat expansion
- [ ] Any outdoor wood structure (deck, pergola, fence) — look for graying or darkening; it's drying out and needs cleaning + sealing before fall

---

## August — Pre-Fall Planning Month

- [ ] **Book your fall wash now** — September/October fills fast with homeowners prepping for winter
- [ ] Walk the roof line from the ground: any missing or lifting shingles?
- [ ] Check chimney cap and flashing if you have a fireplace
- [ ] Look at all exterior paint: blistering or peeling means moisture is getting in — address before winter traps it

---

## September — Fall Prep Begins

**The Most Important Month for Winter Protection**
- [ ] **Fall exterior wash** — removes summer algae, bird droppings, UV residue. A clean surface holds better through winter than a dirty one
- [ ] **Driveway sealing** — do this in September/October before temps drop below 50°F. Sealed concrete resists the freeze-thaw cycle that causes cracking
- [ ] Clean and store outdoor furniture OR cover it thoroughly
- [ ] Inspect weatherstripping on all doors and garage before heating season

---

## October — Winterize

- [ ] Clean gutters after leaves fall — blocked gutters in winter cause ice dams, which cause roof damage and water infiltration
- [ ] Trim any tree branches hanging over the roof (ice storms weigh down branches)
- [ ] If you have a wood deck, apply a final coat of sealer or waterproofing before freeze
- [ ] Inspect exterior faucets and hose bibs — disconnect garden hoses, shut off outdoor water supply
- [ ] Final look at roof: any issues should be addressed now, not in January

---

## November — Shutdown Month

- [ ] Walk the property after first hard freeze — did any new cracks appear in concrete?
- [ ] Check that all downspouts are clear and pointed away from the foundation
- [ ] Note any areas where algae was worst this year (usually shaded north-facing walls) — these need priority attention next spring
- [ ] If you have a driveway, buy sand instead of rock salt for ice — salt eats sealed concrete and kills grass along the edges

---

## December–February — Monitor Only

- [ ] After heavy ice storms: check gutters for ice dam buildup (the long icicles are a warning sign)
- [ ] After big snowfalls: make sure downspouts aren't buried and compacting toward the foundation
- [ ] Book your spring wash in January or February — April/May slots fill up by March in the Cleveland suburbs

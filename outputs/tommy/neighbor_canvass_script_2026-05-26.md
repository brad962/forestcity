## The Opportunity

Every job Bradley books is already deployed at a location. Equipment is there. Truck is parked. The neighbors can SEE the transformation happening in real time. That's the most powerful sales moment of all — they're watching it happen, not reading about it.

**Goal:** Convert 1–2 neighbors on every active job. Zero additional travel, zero ad spend. One sentence per door.

**Revenue math:** If 30% of 20 canvass attempts convert to a quote and 60% of quotes close at $300 avg → 3–4 extra jobs = $900–$1,200 per canvass round at zero marginal cost.

## DOOR KNOCK SCRIPT

**When:** Knock during or right after the job — not before (no before to show yet).

**Who to hit:** 3 houses to the left, 3 to the right, 2 directly across. That's 8 doors in 8 minutes.

**What to say (word for word):**

> "Hey — I'm Bradley with Forest City Power Washing. We're over at [neighbor's address] right now. You can probably see us from here. Wanted to let you know — we've got the equipment here today, so if you want your [driveway/house/roof] done, I can do yours at the same time and save you the travel fee. Normally [price], doing it today while we're already set up — I can do $X."

**The discount:** Take $30–$50 off. Frame it as "same-day, equipment's already here." It's real — no extra drive time for you.

**If they're not home — door hanger or note:**

> "We were here today washing your neighbor's home at [address]. If you want your [driveway/siding] done while we're still in the area, text or call Bradley at [phone]. Same-day discount available. — Forest City Power Washing, Northeast Ohio"

## TEXT VERSION (for neighbors who give their number at the door)

Send SAME DAY after the conversation:

## THREE SCENARIOS

### Scenario A: They say "yes, interested"

> "Great. Takes me about [X hours] on a house this size. I can start right after I'm done at [neighbor's]. You want to do that today?"

If today doesn't work:
> "No problem. I'll come back [day]. I'll send you a text tonight so you have my number."

Log in Workiz as a new lead immediately.

---

### Scenario B: They say "how much?"

Give a range, not a quote. Never quote without seeing it.

> "Depends on the square footage. For a house this size, probably $X–$X for the full exterior. I'll take a quick look right now and give you an exact number — takes 3 minutes."

Then walk around with them. Quoting on the spot with the house visible in front of you closes more than any estimate email.

---

### Scenario C: They say "not right now"

> "No problem. Here's my card — if you change your mind this summer, just text me. We're in this neighborhood a lot."

Leave the card. Don't push. They saw the work. The visual does more selling than you can in 90 seconds.

## AFTER THE JOB — SHARE THE WIN

Post a before/after to Facebook/Instagram that day (or next morning). Tag the neighborhood or city name, not the address.

Caption formula:
> "We were on [City] this weekend. Look at this driveway. The before photo is embarrassing — the after is everything. [CTA]"

This creates social proof in the exact neighborhood where the job happened. Facebook neighborhood targeting means people 2 blocks away will see it in their feed.

## PROBLEM SECTION

### You've Noticed It. So Has Everyone Else.

Those dark streaks running down your shingles? That's not dirt, debris, or age. It's Gloeocapsa Magma — a type of algae that feeds on the limestone in your shingles. It looks like your roof is rotting. And if you leave it long enough, it actually starts to be.

In Northeast Ohio, this is everywhere. The humidity off Lake Erie is basically a petri dish for algae. It comes back every year, like clockwork. Most homeowners either ignore it until they're trying to sell the house, or they wait for an HOA letter to land in the mailbox.

The bigger mistake is trying to fix it yourself — or hiring someone who grabs a pressure washer. High-pressure water on asphalt shingles strips the protective granules right off the surface. Those granules are what protect the shingle from UV damage and water. Once they're gone, your warranty is void and your roof ages twice as fast.

A lot of homeowners don't even know roofs can be professionally cleaned. They think it's a roofer thing, not a washing thing. It's not. Roof soft washing is a real service — and done right, it adds years to the life of your shingles.

## HOW WE DO IT DIFFERENTLY

### Soft Wash Only. No Exceptions.

We don't pressure wash roofs. Not even a little. Not on any setting.

Roof soft washing uses low-pressure water and a biodegradable cleaning solution — the same chemistry used by professional roofers who know what they're doing. It kills the algae at the root instead of blasting the surface. The streaks are gone. The algae is actually dead, not just knocked around. And your shingles are intact.

We've seen what happens when someone hires a guy with a pressure washer to do a roof. You can see the damage from the street — patches where the granules are stripped, sections that look lighter than the rest. It's not fixable. And most manufacturers won't honor a warranty claim after it.

**What we do instead:**

- Apply our low-pressure cleaning solution from the ground or roof edge — no aggressive foot traffic on your shingles
- Let the solution work — it breaks down the algae chemistry, not just the surface layer
- Rinse at low pressure, starting from the ridge down
- Protect your gutters and landscaping throughout the process

**The result:** Clean shingles, no damage, and results that last 2–4 years in Northeast Ohio's climate.

## FAQ

**Q: Will soft washing damage my shingles or void my roof warranty?**
No — and that's actually the opposite of the concern you should have. Pressure washing a roof will void most manufacturer warranties. Soft washing won't. The cleaning solution we use is formulated specifically for asphalt shingles, and the water pressure is far below what would damage the surface. If your warranty has a cleaning clause, soft washing is typically the only approved method.

**Q: I thought only roofers deal with roof problems. Is this the right service?**
It's one of the most common questions we get. Roof cleaning and roof repair are two different things. If your shingles are physically damaged or your flashing is failing, that's a roofer. If your roof looks dark, streaky, or green but is structurally fine — that's an algae problem, and it's something we clean. A lot of roofs that look like they need replacement are just dirty.

**Q: How long will it stay clean?**
In Northeast Ohio, most roofs treated with soft wash stay clean for 2–4 years. The lake humidity means algae grows faster here than in drier climates. Steeper pitches, good sun exposure, and fewer overhanging trees all help. We'll give you an honest read on your specific roof when we quote it.

**Q: Do I need to be home?**
No. We just need access to the roof area and a water source. If you have pets or sensitive plantings close to the house, mention it when you book — we'll take extra care around them. Most customers aren't home during the job and have no issues.

# Estimate Follow-Up Email Template
### Forest City Power Washing — Post-Quote Sequence
*Tommy Greer | Content & Copy Writer*
*Generated: 2026-05-19*

---

Use these after you send an estimate. The goal is to keep Forest City top of mind without being pushy. Three touches max — then let it go or move to a break-up text.

---

## Touch 1: Same day or next day after estimate sent

**Subject:** Your Forest City estimate — quick note

**Body:**
> Hi [First Name],
>
> Just wanted to make sure the estimate came through clearly. A few things worth knowing if you're comparing options:
>
> — We use a soft wash system, not high-pressure. It's gentler on your surfaces and the results hold 2–3 years instead of a few months.
>
> — We're local to [County] — not a national franchise. When something needs a follow-up, you call me directly.
>
> — We're booking 3–4 weeks out right now, so if [Month] works for you, let me know soon.
>
> Any questions on the quote, just reply here or text me at [Bradley's number].
>
> — Bradley
> Forest City Power Washing

**Text version (if they gave you a phone number):**
> "Hey [Name], just wanted to confirm the estimate landed okay. Let me know if you have questions — I'm at [number]. Booking fills up fast this time of year so give me a heads-up when you're ready."

## Touch 2: 3 days after estimate (if no response)

**Subject:** Re: Your Forest City estimate

**Body:**
> Hi [First Name],
>
> Checking in on the estimate I sent over. No pressure — just want to make sure it didn't get buried.
>
> If the price or scope isn't quite right, I'm happy to adjust. And if timing is the issue, I can look at what's available in [Month +1].
>
> What's your thinking at this point?
>
> — Bradley

**Text version:**
> "Hey [Name], Bradley from Forest City. Wanted to follow up on that estimate. Still available if you want to move forward — or happy to answer any questions. What's your thinking?"

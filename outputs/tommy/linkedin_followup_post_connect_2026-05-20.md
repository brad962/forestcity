## The Gap This Fills

The hot lead DM playbook (`hot_lead_linkedin_dm_playbook_2026-05-19.md`) covers the initial connect request.
This file covers what happens NEXT — when they accept.

The connection acceptance is the warmest moment in the whole sequence. Most people miss it.
They accept, you're in their network, and then… nothing. That window closes fast.

**Rule:** Send the follow-up within 24 hours of them accepting. Ideally same day.

## The Follow-Up Messages (Use One — Pick Based on Their Profile)

---

### Version A — Direct, Brief (use for: property managers at management companies)

> Hi [FirstName] — thanks for connecting.
>
> I'm reaching out to property managers in [their area, e.g. "the Akron area"] about power washing services for their properties. We work with a number of HOA and commercial PM firms in Northeast Ohio — spring and summer are when we typically handle prep work before inspections and tenant turnover.
>
> Do you handle exterior maintenance decisions for your properties, or is that someone else on your team? Happy to keep it quick.
>
> — Bradley | Forest City Power Washing

**Why this works:** Direct question gets a response (even "that's not me" is useful). "Before inspections and tenant turnover" speaks their language. Asking who makes the decision lets them refer you internally even if it's not them.

---

### Version B — Warmer, More Conversational (use for: HOA managers, smaller firms)

> Hi [FirstName] — appreciate the connection!
>
> I run Forest City Power Washing out here in Northeast Ohio. We mostly work with property managers and HOA management companies — exterior cleaning, roof soft washing, driveways. The spring rush is in full swing and I've been reaching out to PMs in the area.
>
> Are your properties mostly residential or mixed-use? I'm curious what the exterior maintenance look like on your end this time of year.
>
> — Bradley

**Why this works:** Asking a question about THEIR situation (not pitching) gets responses. People love talking about what they manage. Once they describe their portfolio, you know exactly what to pitch.

---

### Version C — Referral/Relationship Angle (use for: realtors and facility managers)

> Hi [FirstName] — good to be connected.
>
> I saw you're in [real estate / property management] out in [their area]. We do a lot of work for folks in your space — especially pre-listing clean-ups for realtors and exterior maintenance for PMs who need things looking sharp before a showing or inspection.
>
> We're in the middle of our busy season — just curious whether you ever run into situations where a client or property needs exterior cleaning and you're not sure who to call. Happy to be a resource either way.
>
> — Bradley | Forest City Power Washing

**Why this works:** "Happy to be a resource" is low pressure. Opens the door for referrals, not just a direct sale. Realtors especially respond to this framing.

## What to Do With Their Response

**If they say "yes, I handle maintenance":**
→ Immediately pivot to discovery: "Great — what types of properties do you manage mostly? HOA, commercial, apartment?" Then offer a free site estimate.
→ Use the Quote-to-Close Kit: `outputs/tommy/quote_to_close_kit_2026-05-20.md`

**If they say "that's not me, but [colleague] handles that":**
→ Ask for the name. "Oh great — do you happen to know [colleague's] email or LinkedIn? I don't want to bother you with something that's not in your lane." — This is pure gold. Internal referral to the actual decision-maker.

**If they don't respond within 48 hours:**
→ One follow-up, maximum:
> "Hi [FirstName] — just circling back on my last message. No worries if the timing's off — just wanted to make sure it landed. Either way, happy to be connected."
→ After that: no more LinkedIn. Move to phone if they're a Tier 1 hot lead (2+ opens).

**If they say "we're all set" or "not interested":**
→ Be gracious: "No problem at all — appreciate you letting me know. If anything changes on the exterior maintenance side, I'm always around."
→ Mark them "Closed Lost / Not Now" in the pipeline. Don't close the door permanently.

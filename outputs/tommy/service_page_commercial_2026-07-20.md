## HOW WE DO IT DIFFERENTLY

### We Know Commercial Is Different from Residential

**1. We work around your building's schedule.**
We're not going to show up at 9am on a Tuesday and block your tenant parking or your customer entrance for three hours. We schedule jobs to minimize disruption — early mornings, evenings, weekends, or staged by building section. We figure this out with you before we show up.

**2. We use the right method for every surface.**
Brick, EIFS, metal panels, vinyl siding, glass storefronts, concrete, asphalt — each surface needs a different approach. We don't pressure wash everything and call it done. Soft washing for the building envelope, surface-appropriate pressure for concrete and pavement. Using high pressure on the wrong surface causes damage and can strip coatings or void warranties. We know the difference.

**3. We're insured for commercial work.**
If a vendor damages your building or a bystander's property and they're not properly insured, it comes back on you. We carry commercial general liability coverage and can provide certificates of insurance before we set foot on site. That's not optional for us — it's standard.

**4. We document the work.**
Before-and-after photos for your records. We know property managers need to show boards and owners that maintenance was completed. We provide it without being asked.

**5. We're a repeat-service partner, not a one-time call.**
Most commercial properties need exterior cleaning 1–2 times per year. We set up a maintenance schedule that fits your property and your budget cycle, so you're not scrambling to find a vendor every spring.

## FAQ

**Q: How do you handle tenant access and parking during the job?**

We plan around your property's traffic patterns before we show up. Most building washes can be staged by section so we're not shutting down access to the whole building at once. For parking lots or driveways, we coordinate with you on a cone-off schedule that keeps tenant and customer access open as much as possible. We've done this in occupied retail centers and apartment complexes — logistics are part of the job.

**Q: Can you provide a certificate of insurance?**

Yes, every time, without a fight. We carry commercial general liability insurance and can name your management company or property owner as an additional insured if your contract requires it. Send us what you need and we'll get it to you before scheduling.

**Q: We've had vendors damage the building before — what makes you different?**

The most common damage we hear about comes from vendors using high pressure on surfaces that need soft wash. Pressure washing brick, EIFS, or vinyl siding can strip mortar, crack coatings, and drive water into the building envelope. We assess every surface before we start and use the appropriate method. If we see a surface condition that concerns us, we tell you before we touch it — not after the damage is done.

**Q: We need this on a recurring basis — do you offer service agreements?**

Yes. Most of our commercial accounts run on 1–2 visit annual schedules. We work with your maintenance calendar and budget cycle. If your Q3 budget just opened up, we can schedule now and invoice on your timeline. Reach out and we'll put together a schedule that works.

**Q: How do we handle HOA common areas vs. individual units?**

We work with HOA management companies regularly. Common areas — clubhouse exteriors, pool decking, perimeter fencing, signage, entry monuments — are our standard scope. If the association wants to coordinate individual unit cleaning alongside common area work, we can build that into a single property visit to save on mobilization. Talk to us about what your association needs and we'll structure it.

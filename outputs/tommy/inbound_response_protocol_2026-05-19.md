## The 5-Minute Rule

You have **5 minutes**. Research says reply speed is the single biggest driver of whether an inbound lead converts. After 5 minutes, reply rates drop 10x. After 30 minutes, the job is usually gone.

Set your phone up to notify you instantly when:
- A Facebook DM arrives
- A Google Business message comes in
- Your website contact form fires (set up a Zapier → text forward if you haven't)

---

## 4 Types of Inbound — What to Say

### Type 1: Facebook / Google DM — "How much does it cost?"

**Text reply (send within 5 minutes):**
```
Hey [First Name]! Thanks for reaching out. Pricing depends on the size of the house and what surfaces you need done — most homes in the area run between $250 and $500. What's the address? I can give you an accurate number same-day.
```

**If they give the address:**
```
Perfect — I'll look it up and get you a number. Usually takes me about 15 minutes. What's a good number to text you?
```

**Follow-up (if they go quiet after giving the address):**
```
Hey [Name] — looked up [address]. For a house that size I'd estimate [X]. Want me to pencil you in for this week? I have [day] and [day] open.
```

## When They Say "How Soon Can You Come?"

This is a buying signal. Don't hedge.

**Say:**
```
I have [specific day] available. Want to lock that in? I'll send you a text confirmation.
```

**Don't say:**
> "We'll get back to you within 24-48 hours."
> "It depends on our schedule."
> "We'll see what we can do."

When someone asks when you can come, they've already decided to hire you. Just give them a date.

## The Estimate Call (when they need to talk first)

**Opening:**
> "Hey [Name], Bradley from Forest City — thanks for reaching out. I just need to ask you a few quick questions so I can give you an exact number. What's the address?"

**Discovery questions (pick 2-3):**
1. "Is this a full house wash or specific surfaces — siding, roof, driveway?"
2. "How long has it been since it was last cleaned?"
3. "Are you on city water or a well? [Well water = I need to bring my own water]"
4. "Is this before a sale, or just maintenance?"

**The number:**
> "Based on that, I'd put it at around [$X to $Y]. That covers [what's included]. Would you want to go ahead and book it?"

**If they hesitate:**
> "No pressure — I'll send you a text with the number and we can go from there."

## The Cardinal Rules

1. **Use their first name.** Every single time.
2. **Offer specific dates, not vague availability.** "I have Thursday morning" converts better than "sometime next week."
3. **Always ask for the address.** It moves the conversation from "thinking about it" to "this is my house."
4. **Match their energy.** Quick question → quick answer. Don't write three paragraphs when one sentence works.
5. **Text > email for speed.** If you have their number, text first.

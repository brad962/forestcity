# Annual Plan Pitch Script — On-the-Call / On-the-Quote
*Tommy Greer | Forest City Power Washing | 2026-05-26*

---

## When to Use This

Use this EVERY TIME you're quoting a job — whether it's a phone call after a Facebook lead, a callback after a LinkedIn connect, or a walk-around at the property. The annual plan pitch takes 20 seconds. It converts a one-time job into $400–$600/year of recurring revenue with zero additional marketing cost.

Best moment: **after they say yes to the quote price, before you hang up.**

---

## The 20-Second Annual Plan Pitch

> "One thing I want to mention — we do an annual maintenance plan where we come out twice a year, spring and fall. It keeps everything from getting built up so it's never a big job again. Most customers do it for around [price range — $399–$499 depending on property size]. A lot of people just set it and forget it. Would that be something you'd want to hear more about?"

**Stop talking. Wait for their answer.**

---

## Response Handling

**If they say YES or "tell me more":**
> "So basically what we do is schedule your spring visit around late April or May before the algae really sets in, and then we come back in October before winter. Both visits are the full soft wash — house, roof, driveway, whatever you had done today. The price locks in, so even if we raise rates next year, you're covered. I'll send you a quick email that lays it out. What's the best email for you?"

→ Send booking_confirmation_text with annual plan pricing noted
→ Follow up with annual_plan_customer_welcome_kit email within 24h

---

**If they say "how much?":**
> "Depends on what you want included — most people do the full house wash both visits for around $450 a year. Some add the driveway and it's closer to $550. Either way it works out cheaper than booking twice separately, and you don't have to remember to call us."

---

**If they say "maybe later" or "let me think about it":**
> "No problem at all. I'll include a note about it in the invoice — if you decide you want to add it before your spring visit next year, just let me know."

→ Add to pipeline_data.json note: "Annual plan follow-up — autumn 2026"

---

**If they say NO:**
> "Totally fine — plenty of people just book when they need it. We'll take great care of you today. I'll send you a reminder in the fall if you want."

→ Move on. Don't push. One-time customers often convert after the first job anyway.

---

## The Revenue Math (for your own context)

- 20 customers say YES to $450/year plan = **$9,000 recurring annual revenue — zero additional marketing spend**
- That's $4,500 that shows up before June is over (spring visits) and $4,500 in October (fall visits)
- Locks in cash flow in your slowest months (October–November before winter)
- Each customer on the plan is 10x less likely to get a quote from a competitor — they're already committed

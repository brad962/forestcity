# Post-Sequence Recovery Protocol
### What Bradley Does After Touch 3 Fires (May 22)
*Tommy Greer | Content & Copy | Written 2026-05-20*

---

## The Situation

Touch 3 fires Friday May 22. After that, your 45 Mixmax contacts are no longer in any automated sequence. They just... stop getting emails. Most won't have replied. That doesn't mean they're dead — it means the automation did its job (warmed them up) and now it's your turn.

The contacts with 2+ opens are the gold. They read your stuff. They just didn't click the reply button. That's not rejection — that's hesitation. A personal, human touch breaks hesitation.

---

## Who to Target First

**Priority 1 — Hot (2+ opens, no reply):** ~13 contacts. These are warm leads. They know your name and have read your email multiple times. A LinkedIn connect or personal email from Bradley's Gmail (not Mixmax) will land completely differently.

**Priority 2 — Opened once, no reply:** ~6 contacts. Worth one more personal attempt. 1 LinkedIn message or 1 text if you have the number.

**Priority 3 — Never opened:** ~26 contacts. These are likely soft-bounce, spam-filtered, or genuinely not interested. Don't re-contact via email. Archive them. Worth one LinkedIn search to see if the person is findable — if so, connect there.

---

## The Play — 48 Hours After Touch 3

### Step 1: LinkedIn Warmup (Friday May 22 — while Touch 3 is sending)
Send connection requests to the 13 hot leads. Use this note (40 words max):

> *"Hi [first name] — I've been reaching out via email about power washing for your properties. Wanted to connect here too in case email's not the best way to reach you. Happy to answer any questions."*

**Why it works:** They get Touch 3 AND a LinkedIn connect the same day. Two different channels = feels more real, less automated. The LinkedIn note references the email, which ties it all together.

---

### Step 2: Personal Gmail Email (Saturday May 23 or Monday May 26)
For the top 5 hot leads (highest open count), send a 3-sentence Gmail email. No Mixmax, no tracking, no automation.

**Subject:** Quick question — [their company name]

**Body:**
> Hey [first name],
>
> I sent a few emails over the past few weeks about power washing your properties — just wanted to reach out personally before summer gets busy. I know your schedule is packed, so I'll make it simple: can I stop by one of your properties for a 10-minute walk-through quote?
>
> No commitment. Just want to show you the difference.
>
> — Bradley
> Forest City Power Washing
> [phone number]

**Why this works:** Short. Personal. Specific ask (walk-through quote, not "schedule a call"). Doesn't mention the previous automated emails. Feels like a neighbor, not a campaign.

---

### Step 3: Text the Contractors (Monday May 26 — first business day after Memorial Day)
The manual pipeline contractors in "New Lead" stage — the ones who have phone numbers — should get a text on May 26. This is the highest-response window:

> *"Hey [first name], it's Bradley from Forest City Power Washing. We do referral partnerships with contractors in the area — $50 per job you send our way. Interested in a quick 5-minute call this week?"*

Templates and full list: `outputs/vera/sms_templates_contractors_2026-05-18.md`
Priority list: `outputs/vera/priority_outreach_list_2026-05-19.md`

---

### Step 4: Archive Non-Openers
Contacts who never opened a single email after 3 touches:
1. Export them from Mixmax
2. Search them on LinkedIn
3. If you find them: connect with a note (see template above). Skip the email history.
4. If not on LinkedIn: add to a "Fall Re-engage" list. Try again in September with a new message and a different angle (pre-winter cleaning, protect the investment).

Don't delete them. Just park them.

## Tracking (Simple — No HubSpot Needed)

Add a note to pipeline_data.json for each contact:
- `"notes": "Touch 3 sent 5/22. LinkedIn connect sent 5/22. Gmail sent 5/26."`
- `"stage": "Contacted"`
- `"last_contact": "2026-05-26"`

If they reply to any channel: move to "Replied" and schedule an estimate call.

---

## Results to Watch For

- A LinkedIn acceptance = warm signal. Message them immediately.
- A Gmail reply = HOT lead. Call them same day.
- An estimate call booked = win. This is the entire goal.

One PM account in NE Ohio = 2-15 properties. One deal from this sequence = $500–$5,000+ in recurring annual revenue.

## 1. Facebook Comment Response

Someone comments "interested" or "how much?" on a post.

**Immediate reply (public comment — visible to everyone):**
> "Hey [Name]! Great — DM us with your address and what you're looking to have done and we'll get you a quick estimate. Usually same day. 🙌"

Then immediately send them a DM (don't wait for them to respond publicly):
> "Hey [Name], it's Bradley from Forest City Power Washing. Saw you commented on our post — what's the address and what are you looking to have done? House wash, roof, driveway, deck? I can usually turn around a number pretty fast."

---

**If they comment "how much?" (price question in comments):**

Don't give a number in the comments. Too much variation by house size/condition. Instead:

Public reply:
> "Prices vary a lot by square footage and what needs doing — DM us or text us at [phone] and I'll give you a real number fast. Most house washes run $200–$500 depending on size."

**Note:** Giving a rough range ($200-$500) in the comment anchors expectations and filters tire-kickers. You can adjust this range based on your current pricing.

## 2. Facebook DM Response (Organic)

Someone DMs after seeing a post or being referred.

**Opening script (first message back):**
> "Hey [Name]! It's Bradley. Thanks for reaching out. What's the address and what are you looking to have cleaned — house, roof, driveway, deck? I'll get you a number."

**After they give address:**
> "Got it — [neighborhood/city]. Based on what you're describing, I'm looking at roughly $[X]–$[X] for [service]. That includes [brief description]. Does that range work for you, or do you want me to swing by and give you an exact quote?"

**If they ask for a site visit:**
> "Works for me. What's your schedule like? I can usually get there in the next day or two. Morning or afternoon better for you?"

## 4. Inbound Call Script

Someone calls (from seeing a Facebook post, GBP listing, neighbor referral, or yard sign).

**Answer:**
> "Forest City Power Washing, this is Bradley."

**If they say "I saw your Facebook / a friend recommended you / I found you on Google":**
> "Thanks for calling. What are you looking to have done and what's the address?"

**Discovery questions (ask these, in this order):**
1. "And what's the biggest issue — is it the siding, the roof, the driveway, or all of it?"
2. "When are you looking to get this done?" *(urgency check)*
3. "Is this a house you live in or a rental / commercial property?" *(scope check)*

**Quoting on the phone:**
- House wash (2,000 sq ft average): $250–$350
- House wash (larger or two-story): $350–$500
- Roof soft wash: $300–$500 (single story), $500–$800 (two-story or steep)
- Driveway: $100–$200
- Deck/fence: $150–$350
- Full combo (house + roof + drive): often $600–$1,000+, offer a bundle discount

> "Based on what you're describing, I'm probably looking at [range]. That's all-in, no hidden fees. Does that work for you?"

**Closing:**
> "Great. Let me get you on the schedule. What's your availability — morning or afternoon better for you? I'm booking [timeframe]."

**If they want to think about it:**
> "No problem. I'll text you right now so you have my number. When you're ready, just reply. We're booking fast so the sooner the better for scheduling."

Then text them within 60 seconds:
> "Hey [Name], it's Bradley from Forest City Power Washing. Good talking to you. Here if you have questions — just reply to this text. [your number]"

## 5. Missed Call Recovery

If you miss a call (no voicemail, or they didn't leave one):

**Call back within 5 minutes:**
> "Hey — this is Bradley from Forest City Power Washing. Sorry I missed you. What can I help you with?"

**If no answer on callback, leave voicemail:**
> "Hey [Name], it's Bradley from Forest City Power Washing — you called a few minutes ago. Give me a call back at [number] or shoot me a text and I'll get right back to you. Thanks."

**Then immediately text:**
> "Hey — it's Bradley from Forest City Power Washing. You called a few minutes ago. Tried calling you back. What can I help you with? Just reply here."

## 6. Facebook DM — When They Go Cold

They DM'd, you replied, no response after 24 hours.

**Follow-up (once, 24-48h later):**
> "Hey [Name] — just wanted to make sure this didn't get buried. Still happy to get you a quote for [what they mentioned]. We're booking into [month] now. Let me know!"

If no response after that, leave it. Don't send a third unprompted DM — comes across as pushy.

## 2. Soft Wash Roof Cleaning

**Page slug:** /roof-soft-wash

**H1:**
That Black Streaking on Your Roof Isn't Age. It's Alive.

**Subheadline:**
Gloeocapsa magma — a bacteria — is slowly destroying your shingles. We kill it. One treatment. Results that last 3–5 years.

**Body:**

If your roof has dark streaks running down from the ridge, you have a bacteria problem. It's called Gloeocapsa magma, and it feeds on the limestone in asphalt shingles. Left alone, it shortens your roof's life by 5–10 years.

Pressure washing won't fix it. It damages the shingles and the bacteria grows back in 6 months.

Soft wash roof cleaning uses a low-pressure application of a cleaning solution that kills the bacteria at the source. The streaks wash off. The bacteria is dead. Your roof looks 10 years newer.

**Who needs this:**
- Any roof with dark streaks, black patches, or moss
- Homes that have been on the market and need curb appeal
- Property managers with multi-unit buildings (we do commercial volume)
- Homeowners who want to extend their roof's life without a $15,000 replacement

**What we use:**
Sodium hypochlorite solution (same chemistry professional restoration companies use), applied at low pressure so there's zero shingle damage. Safe for landscaping when applied correctly — and we take care of it.

**Pricing:**
Most residential roofs run $350–$600 depending on pitch and size. We'll quote it on-site. Free, same-week.

**FAQ block:**

**"Won't the chemicals hurt my plants?"**
We wet down plants and landscaping before we start and rinse after. We've done this on hundreds of homes — never had plant damage when we follow the right procedure.

**"How long does it last?"**
3–5 years for most roofs. Northeast Ohio's humidity means the bacteria comes back eventually, but not quickly.

**"My neighbor said pressure washing is fine."**
It removes the visual buildup temporarily. But high pressure on asphalt granules accelerates wear and voids most manufacturer warranties. Soft wash is the industry-standard method recommended by ARMA (Asphalt Roofing Manufacturers Association).

Wood decks and fences in Northeast Ohio go gray fast. The culprit is a combination of UV exposure, moisture, and organic growth — black and green mildew that works into the grain. Left alone long enough, it's not just ugly, it's structural.

## SCENARIO 1 — "Interested / Tell me more"

**Their reply:** "Hey, what do you charge?" / "Tell me more about what you do." / "I might be interested."

**Your response:**

> Hey [First Name] — glad you reached out. We're a power washing company based out of Northeast Ohio. We handle house washing, roof soft washing, driveways, decks, and commercial buildings.
>
> Most residential jobs run $250–$600 depending on size. Commercial varies by square footage.
>
> What's the property look like? I can give you a number same-day if you shoot me an address.
>
> — Bradley
> Forest City Power Washing | (your number)

**Note:** Ask for the address, not "can I schedule a call." Get the info to give a number. People who ask price are buyers.

---

## SCENARIO 2 — "Want a quote / Schedule me"

**Their reply:** "Can you give me a quote?" / "When can you come out?" / "We have a property that needs work."

**Your response:**

> Absolutely. What's the address? I'll take a look on Google Street View and give you a ballpark same-day — no need to schedule a visit for the initial quote.
>
> If the property is larger or commercial, I'll usually want to swing by, but for most residential jobs I can do it from photos.
>
> What are you looking to get done — full house, driveway, roof?
>
> — Bradley

**Note:** Eliminate friction. Don't make them wait for a visit. Give a range from photos/address first.

---

## SCENARIO 3 — "Not right now / Too busy" (soft no)

**Their reply:** "We're good for now." / "Not in the budget right now." / "Maybe later in the summer."

**Your response (keep it short):**

> No problem at all, [First Name]. Reach out whenever it makes sense — we're typically booking 2–3 weeks out during peak season so the earlier the better.
>
> Have a great Memorial Day weekend.
>
> — Bradley

**Note:** No pressure. Leave the door wide open. These often convert 4–6 weeks later when they see the neighbors' house.

---

## SCENARIO 4 — "Wrong person / Removed"

**Their reply:** "I don't handle this." / "Please remove me."

**Your response:**

> Totally understand — I'll take you off the list. If you know who handles exterior maintenance for the properties, I'd appreciate the referral. Either way, have a great one.
>
> — Bradley

**Note:** Always ask for a referral on unsubscribes. 1 in 10 will name someone.

---

## SCENARIO 5 — Property Manager: "We already have a vendor"

**Their reply:** "We use (company name) for this."

**Your response:**

> Good to know — appreciate you letting me know. When that relationship comes up for review, keep us in mind. We work with several property management companies in [County] and our rates are competitive.
>
> Mind if I check back with you in the fall?
>
> — Bradley

**Note:** Ask for permission to follow up. "Mind if I check back in the fall?" gets a yes 80% of the time, and you've now added them to your fall pipeline.

---

## SCENARIO 6 — Contractor / Referral Partner Reply

**Their reply:** "What's this about?" / "We don't need power washing."

**Your response:**

> Hey [First Name] — should have been clearer. I wasn't reaching out as a customer — I'm looking to build a referral partnership.
>
> We're Forest City Power Washing out of Northeast Ohio. A lot of our customers need [their trade] work done, and vice versa. We've set up simple referral deals with a few [painters/landscapers/roofers] in the area — $50 per job that closes from a referral, both ways.
>
> Worth a 5-minute chat sometime?
>
> — Bradley

**Note:** Reframe immediately. Contractors often don't read past the subject line and assume it's a sales pitch TO them.

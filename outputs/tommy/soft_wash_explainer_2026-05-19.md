## Version 2: Website Copy (FAQ or Service Page)

### Soft Wash vs. Pressure Wash: What's the Difference?

**Pressure washing** uses high-force water — typically 1,500–3,000 PSI — to physically blast dirt, debris, and staining off surfaces. It works well on concrete: driveways, sidewalks, and patios can handle the force.

**Soft washing** uses low pressure (under 500 PSI) combined with a biodegradable cleaning solution to break down and kill organic growth — algae, mold, mildew, and lichen — at the root. The solution does the cleaning. The rinse just carries it away.

**Why does it matter?**

The staining you see on NE Ohio homes — that green-black discoloration on siding and roofs — is almost always algae and mold, not dirt. Pressure alone knocks it off the surface, but the organism is still alive in the material. It grows back in 6–8 weeks.

Soft wash kills it. That's why a soft wash treatment lasts 2–3 years where a high-pressure blast lasts a season.

**For roofs: soft wash only.**

High-pressure washing strips the granules from asphalt shingles, shortening roof life and voiding most manufacturer warranties. We never pressure wash roofs. Every roof cleaning we do is soft wash — low pressure, the right solution, safe for shingles.

**For concrete: pressure wash or soft wash depends on the job.**

Driveways, walkways, and patios handle high pressure well. For heavily stained or contaminated concrete, we'll sometimes combine a cleaning solution pre-treatment with a pressure rinse for the best result.

## PROBLEM SECTION

### You're Not the Only One Who's Put This Off

Here's what happens in Northeast Ohio: the lake humidity kicks in, the winter ends, and by May your north-facing siding is covered in green slime. The fascia looks gray. The gutters have dark streaks running down from the corners. The foundation is black.

It doesn't happen overnight, so you stop noticing it. Then someone pulls into your driveway and you see the house through their eyes — and it's been looking like that for two years.

It's not permanent damage. It's algae and mold. And it comes off.

The problem with trying to fix it yourself: most people either rent a pressure washer and blast the paint off (the algae comes back in six weeks because you killed the top layer, not the root), or they spray it with a garden hose and nothing happens. Neither of those is soft washing — and soft washing is what actually works on siding.

---

## HOW WE DO IT DIFFERENTLY

### Soft Wash — Not Just a Pressure Washer on Low

We don't point a pressure washer at your house and call it done.

Soft washing uses low-pressure water and a biodegradable cleaning solution that kills the algae, mold, and bacteria at the root — not just knocking it off the surface. That's why results last 2–3 years instead of 6 months.

High pressure on vinyl siding drives water behind the panels and into the wall cavity. That causes rot and mold that you can't see. High pressure on painted wood strips the finish. High pressure on older caulk lines breaks the seal.

We've been doing this long enough to know: the right method for siding is always low pressure, always the right solution, and always checking the surface first.

**What that means for you:**
- The green comes off and stays off longer
- No damage to paint, caulk, trim, or window seals
- We rinse the landscaping before and after — the solution is safe, but we're careful anyway
- You don't have to be home. Just let us know the dogs are inside.

## FAQ

**Q: Will soft washing damage my vinyl siding?**
No. Vinyl siding is designed for it. The cleaning solution we use is biodegradable and the water pressure is lower than a garden hose nozzle on the strong setting. What damages siding is a pressure washer on high — that drives water under the panels. We never do that on siding.

**Q: How long does it take?**
Most 3-bedroom single-story homes take 2–3 hours. A two-story with detached garage might run 4 hours. We'll give you an honest time estimate when you call so you can plan your day.

**Q: How long will it stay clean?**
In Northeast Ohio, most homes need a wash every 2–3 years. The lake humidity means algae grows faster here than in drier parts of the country. If you're in a low-lying area with a lot of trees close to the house, you might see it come back sooner. We'll be honest with you about that when we see the property.

**Q: Do I need to do anything to prepare?**
Close your windows and patio doors. If you have low-planted flowers right against the foundation, just mention it — we'll extra-rinse that area. You don't need to move furniture or be home unless you want to be.

**Q: I tried a pressure washer and it came back in a few weeks. Why would yours be different?**
Pressure only knocks the algae off the surface. Our solution actually kills it — the same way bleach kills mold in a bathroom instead of just scrubbing. That's why pressure-only results fade fast and soft wash results last 2–3 years.

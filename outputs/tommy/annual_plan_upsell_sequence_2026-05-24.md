# Annual Plan Upsell Sequence
### Tommy Greer | Copy & Scripts | 2026-05-24
*Send within 2 hours of job completion while satisfaction is peak. Target: every residential job.*

---

## Why This Matters
One-time job average: $175–$300. Annual plan: $400–$600/year for 2 visits (spring + pre-winter).
At 10 annual customers = $4,000–$6,000 locked revenue before a single new lead is needed.
Conversion is highest in the 2-hour window after the job — the house looks incredible and they just saw the before/after.

---

## STEP 1 — Same-Day Text (send within 2 hours of job completion)

Send from Bradley's phone, personally. NOT a bulk blast.

**Version A — direct offer:**
> "Hey [FirstName], it's Bradley from Forest City. Hope you love how it looks! We do a spring wash + pre-winter clean every year for a lot of our customers — $[X]/yr, we just show up and handle it both times. Want me to add you to the schedule? No commitment, you can cancel anytime."

**Version B — lead with a compliment + soft offer:**
> "Hey [FirstName], the house looks great — really popped once the algae came off. Quick question: would it be helpful to do this automatically every spring and fall? We offer an annual plan, $[X]/yr for 2 visits. Just reply YES and I'll lock you in."

**Pricing guide for the text:**
- House wash only: $350–$450/yr
- House + driveway: $450–$550/yr
- House + driveway + deck: $525–$625/yr
- House + roof soft wash: $525–$650/yr
- Full Showstopper (all surfaces): $575–$700/yr

**If they ask for specifics:** "Based on what we did today, you'd be looking at $[X]–$[Y]/yr. I can lock that rate in for you now — it won't go up as long as you stay on the plan."

---

## STEP 2 — Response Handling

**If YES:**
→ Reply: "Perfect, I'll get you on the books. You'll get a reminder text 3 days before each visit. First one next spring, second one late October. Thanks [FirstName]!"
→ Log in Workiz with tag "Annual Plan"
→ Send the welcome kit: `outputs/tommy/annual_plan_customer_welcome_kit_2026-05-24.md`

**If "how much exactly?" / "send me more info":**
→ Reply: "Sure — for your property it would be $[specific price] for 2 visits (spring wash + pre-winter clean). Same crew, same results. Want me to lock that in?"
→ Don't over-explain. Keep it short.

**If "let me think about it" / no reply by end of day:**
→ Move to Step 3 (30-day follow-up email)

**If NO:**
→ Reply: "No worries at all! I'll send a reminder next spring and you can decide then."
→ Tag them "Past Customer — No Annual Plan" in Workiz for next year's text

## Objection Handling

**"I'll just call when I need it."**
> "Totally fair. I'll make a note to reach out next spring — but the plan locks in this year's price, which saves $40–$60 vs. booking on your own. Up to you."

**"Too expensive."**
> "Understood. To put it another way: one visit a year runs $[one-time price]. The plan gets you two visits for $[annual price] — so the second visit is basically $[difference]. That's why most customers say yes."

**"We might sell the house."**
> "Even better — fresh exterior cleaning before listing adds value and photos better. If you sell, you just cancel the plan. No penalty."

**"I need to check with my wife/husband."**
> "Of course. I'll send you a quick text summary you can share with them. No rush — take a few days."
> [Follow up in 3 days: "Hey [FirstName], just checking if you got a chance to chat about the annual plan. Happy to answer any questions."]

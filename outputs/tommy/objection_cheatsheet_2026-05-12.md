## How to Use This

Keep this handy when you're on the phone or at a door. These aren't scripts — they're conversation handles. Say it in your own words, but hit these points.

---

## Objection 1: "I can get it cheaper on Facebook."

**What they're really saying:** I'm price-shopping and want to feel like I'm getting a deal.

**Your response:**
> "You absolutely can find cheaper guys on Facebook, and some of them are solid. The risk is the ones who don't show up, don't carry insurance, and you have no idea what they're spraying on your siding. We're not trying to be the cheapest option — we're the ones who show up when we say we will, do the job right, and stand behind it. If you get burned by someone else, call us. We'll fix it."

**Key points:**
- Don't trash the competition — just raise the risk
- Anchor on reliability and insurance (these are real fears homeowners have)
- Leave the door open: "call us if the other guy doesn't show"

---

## Objection 2: "I'll just rent a pressure washer and do it myself."

**What they're really saying:** I'm handy and don't want to pay for something I think I can do.

**Your response:**
> "For your driveway or sidewalk? That totally makes sense — go for it. But if you're thinking about your siding or your roof, a pressure washer will actually cause damage. It drives water behind the panels and causes rot behind the siding. On a roof, it strips the granules off your shingles and voids your warranty — we've seen that happen. Soft washing is the only safe method for those surfaces, and it actually kills the algae and mold at the root instead of just blasting it around."

**Key points:**
- Validate them for concrete/hard surfaces (don't fight a winnable argument)
- Switch the frame to siding/roof specifically
- The warranty void is a powerful anchor — it's a real consequence
- "Kills at the root" vs. "blasting it around" — this is a vivid distinction

---

## Objection 3: "I don't need it that often."

**What they're really saying:** I don't see the urgency. Why should I do this now?

**Your response:**
> "That's fair — it doesn't look bad every year. But here in Northeast Ohio, with the humidity coming off the lake, algae and mold are back within 12-18 months. By the time you actually see the black streaks on your roof, you're already into potential shingle damage. The algae is feeding on the limestone in your shingles. Most of our regular customers do it every year or every other year. Our annual maintenance plan gives you two visits — spring and pre-winter — at a discount, so you just set it and forget it."

**Key points:**
- Northeast Ohio + lake humidity = faster growth than they expect
- By the time it's visible, there's already damage — shift from cosmetic to structural
- Bridge to the annual plan naturally

---

## Objection 4: "We had it done two years ago."

**What they're really saying:** I don't feel like it needs to be done again yet.

**Your response:**
> "Nice — you're ahead of most people. Given how wet our springs are in Northeast Ohio, two years is actually right around when the algae starts making a comeback. If you're seeing any green tint on the north side or any dark streaks starting on the roof, that's the signal. We can swing by and take a look — no charge for the estimate."

**Key points:**
- Validate that they're being proactive (don't make them feel stupid)
- 2 years is legitimate timing in NE Ohio — reinforce that science
- Drop the free estimate — low friction, gets foot in door

---

## Objection 5: "It's not in the budget right now."

**What they're really saying:** I might want it, but I need permission to spend the money — or a reason to prioritize it.

**Your response:**
> "Totally get it — it's not an emergency buy. What I'd say is the Fresh Start package starts at $149, which handles one surface. If the driveway or the front of the house is what's bothering you most, we can start there. Or we can put you on the list for a couple months out when things free up — I just want to make sure you're not dealing with a more expensive repair later because of algae or mold getting under the siding."

**Key points:**
- Offer the entry-level option — lower the barrier
- Future-date it (put them on a list — low pressure, keeps the lead warm)
- Plant the seed: "more expensive repair later" — protect the investment framing

---

## Bonus: When They Go Silent After Getting a Quote

Follow-up message (text or email):

> "Hey [Name], just following up on the estimate for the [service]. Still happy to get that scheduled for you. Spring's filling up fast and I want to make sure I can get you a slot before the rush. Just reply here or give me a call. — Bradley, Forest City Power Washing"

**Why it works:**
- Personal (use their name, mention the specific service)
- Scarcity is real — spring IS the busy season
- Easy response path
- Signed from Bradley (humanizes it)

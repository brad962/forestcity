## THE PROBLEM

### You're Out There All Summer. Your Deck Shouldn't Look Like This.

July in Northeast Ohio means you're finally using your backyard. Cookouts, kids in the yard, neighbors over on weekends. And every time you step out the back door, you see it: the green stuff working its way across the fence boards. The deck that's gone gray and weathered. The algae creeping up from the grass line.

It's not just ugly — it's slippery, it's embarrassing, and if you're thinking about staining or resealing the deck, none of that works until the surface is clean.

Here's the problem with wood: most people grab a pressure washer and go to town. That strips the wood fibers, raises the grain, and can force water into the joints and cause rot. You end up with a deck that looks worse than before and now needs sanding. We've gotten calls from exactly those customers.

Soft washing is the right method for wood. Lower pressure, the right cleaning solution, and the wood stays intact.

## HOW WE DO IT DIFFERENTLY

### We Don't Just Blast It. We Actually Clean It.

**1. We use soft wash, not high pressure.**
High-pressure washing on wood is a mistake — it raises the grain, splinters the surface, and can drive water deep into the boards. We use low-pressure application with a biodegradable cleaning solution that kills the algae, mold, and mildew at the root. It doesn't just knock the green stuff off the surface — it kills it so it doesn't come back in three weeks.

**2. We match the method to the surface.**
Deck boards, railings, lattice, fence pickets, and posts each have different wood types and conditions. We adjust the mix and the approach based on what we're looking at — not a one-size-fits-all spray.

**3. We handle the detail work.**
Between the boards. The post bases. The fence line at ground level where the algae always starts. We don't call it done because the main surface looks clean. The edges and the bottom of the structure are where the problem lives — and that's where we work.

**4. We set you up for the next step.**
If you're planning to stain or seal the deck, we'll tell you exactly how long to let the wood dry before applying product. A clean, prepped surface means the stain bonds properly and lasts twice as long. A lot of staining contractors recommend us specifically for this reason.

## FAQ

**Q: Can't I just pressure wash my own deck?**

You can, and plenty of people try it. The problem is that consumer pressure washers run too high for most wood surfaces — especially older or softer wood. You'll raise the grain, splinter the boards, and possibly cause damage that opens the wood to rot. If you've already done this and the deck looks rougher than when you started, that's a common call we get. Soft washing is safer and the results actually last because you're killing the organism, not just washing the surface.

**Q: What's the green stuff on my fence — is it dangerous?**

That's algae and/or mold. In Northeast Ohio, the combination of Lake Erie humidity and shade on north-facing fences creates the perfect condition for it. It's not dangerous in small amounts, but it spreads, it makes the wood slippery, and it breaks down the wood fibers over time if you don't treat it. Getting rid of it now is cheaper than replacing fence boards in two or three years.

**Q: I want to stain my deck this summer. Should I wash it first?**

Yes — and it matters a lot. Stain applied to a dirty or moldy surface doesn't bond properly. It'll peel, bubble, or fade within a season. Most deck staining contractors won't touch a deck that hasn't been cleaned first. We can clean it, tell you how long to wait for proper drying (usually 48–72 hours in summer weather), and you'll get a stain job that actually lasts.

**Q: Is the soft wash solution safe for my plants and grass?**

Yes. We use biodegradable cleaning solutions and we rinse the surrounding area before and after the application. We've done hundreds of Northeast Ohio backyards — landscaping, flower beds, garden borders — without damage. If you have specific plants you're worried about, tell us when you book and we'll talk through it.

**Q: How long does it take?**

A standard deck takes 1–2 hours. Add a fence run and you're typically looking at 2–3 hours total depending on size. We'll give you a realistic time estimate when you book so you know when to plan to be home or away. Most customers don't need to be there at all once access is set.

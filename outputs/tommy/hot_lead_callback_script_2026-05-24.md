# Hot Lead Callback Script — Tommy Greer
### Forest City Power Washing
*For use when a PM, realtor, or contractor who opened your email calls back UNPROMPTED*
*Created 2026-05-24*

---

## Why This Script Is Different From Cold Calling

When someone who opened your email 2–3 times calls you back, the dynamic is completely different:
- **They already know what you do.** Don't explain the company.
- **They have intent.** The only question is whether the timing and price work.
- **They're testing you.** Your professionalism in this 3-minute call determines if they book or ghost.
- **Don't oversell.** They already decided you're worth a call — your job is to confirm you're easy to work with.

The biggest mistake: treating this like a cold call. Don't pitch. Don't explain. Just ask and confirm.

---

## Opening (first 15 seconds — sets the whole tone)

> "Hey [Name], this is Bradley from Forest City Power Washing — thanks for calling me back."

Then **stop talking.** Let them lead.

**If they say "I got your email":**
> "Great — yeah, I wanted to reach out to [property managers / realtors / contractors] in [county]. What was the property you were thinking about?"

**If they say "Is this about power washing?":**
> "Yeah, Forest City Power Washing — we do exterior cleaning across Northeast Ohio. I sent you an email a few days ago. What's the situation on your end?"

**If they seem unsure why they called:**
> "No worries — I'll make it easy. I emailed you about cleaning services for [your properties / your listings / your clients]. Do you have a property or a job that needs some work?"

---

## The 3 Discovery Questions (ask all 3, in this order)

Once they're talking, ask these. Each one tells you something important.

### Question 1: What do they have?
> "What kind of property are we talking about — [single property / a few listings / a portfolio of buildings]?"

- **Property manager:** Listening for number of units, type (HOA, apartments, commercial)
- **Realtor:** Listening for if it's a current listing or upcoming
- **Contractor:** Listening for job type (pre-paint wash, deck, driveway) and frequency

### Question 2: What's the problem they're seeing?
> "What's the situation with the exterior right now — is it roof staining, siding, driveway, or everything?"

- This tells you scope and gives you the pricing anchor
- Common NE Ohio answers: "the roof looks black," "green algae on the siding," "driveway is stained," "whole thing needs it"

### Question 3: What's the timeline?
> "Is this something you're looking to get done soon, or are you planning ahead?"

- **"Soon"** = they've been sitting on it, ready to move. Quote fast.
- **"Planning ahead"** = pre-season or pre-listing. Warm lead — follow up in 2 weeks.

---

## How to Quote on the Phone (don't be vague — give a range)

After question 2, you have enough to quote a range. Be direct. Don't say "I'd have to come see it." Say this:

> "Based on what you're describing, for a [single-family / multi-unit / commercial building] with [roof / siding / driveway], I'd be looking at somewhere in the range of [$X to $Y]. I can confirm the number when I see the property — but that's probably the ballpark."

**Reference ranges:**
- House wash (siding only, avg size): $200–$350
- House + driveway: $300–$500
- Full exterior (house + roof + driveway): $450–$700
- Commercial/multi-unit: call at $150–$250 per building, quote individually
- Roof soft wash only: $250–$450 depending on size and pitch

---

## How to Close (set the next step, don't leave it open)

> "Here's what I'd suggest — let me come by and take a quick look. Takes 15 minutes. I'll send you a written quote same day, and you can decide from there. Does [day/time] work for you?"

**If they hesitate:**
> "No obligation on the walkthrough — I just want to make sure the number I give you is accurate. Easier than going back and forth."

**If they want to book without a walkthrough (smaller jobs):**
> "I can lock you in without the walkthrough for a job this size — I'll just send you a confirmation with the price and we'll schedule the crew. Does [date] work?"

---

## Handling "Can You Send Me Something in Writing?"

This is the most common response. Don't email them a PDF and wait. Do this:

> "Absolutely — I'll send over a quick proposal today. But let me ask: is there anything that would stop you from moving forward if the price looks right?"

**Why:** Forces the real objection to surface before you waste time on a proposal.

- If they say "no, just need to see the number" → send a 2-line email with the price and a booking link.
- If they say "I need to talk to my HOA board" → "Totally — when's your next board meeting? I'll have something ready by then."
- If they say "I'm also getting a couple other quotes" → "Fair enough — what does your timeline look like for making a decision?" (then follow up the day before).

## The 60-Second Version (if you need a quick cheat sheet)

1. **Open:** "Thanks for calling back." Then shut up.
2. **3 Qs:** What property? What problem? What timeline?
3. **Range:** Give a number. "$X to $Y is probably the ballpark."
4. **Close:** "Let me come by for 15 minutes. I'll send the number same day."
5. **After:** Log it. Follow-up email in 30 min.

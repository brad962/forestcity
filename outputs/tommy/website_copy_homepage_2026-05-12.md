### HOW WE'RE DIFFERENT SECTION

**Headline:** Not All Power Washing Is the Same

**Subhead:** We use soft wash for most surfaces — and here's why that matters.

**Body:**
A standard pressure washer runs 2,500–4,000 PSI. That's enough to etch concrete, strip paint, and damage siding and roof shingles permanently.

Soft washing uses a fraction of the pressure with a professional-grade cleaning solution that kills algae, mold, and bacteria at the root — not just the surface. The result lasts 4–6 times longer than a standard pressure wash.

For roofs especially, soft wash isn't just better — it's the only safe method. High-pressure roof washing voids most manufacturer warranties. We don't do that.

### FAQ SECTION

**Is soft washing safe for my roof?**
Yes — soft washing is actually the only *safe* way to clean a roof. High-pressure washing can loosen shingles, force water under flashing, and void manufacturer warranties. Soft wash uses low pressure with a professional cleaning solution. Your shingles won't know we were there — except the algae is gone.

**Will it damage my siding?**
No. We adjust pressure and solution based on your siding material — vinyl, wood, stucco, brick, or fiber cement. We've cleaned hundreds of homes across Northeast Ohio and know what each surface needs.

**How long does it take?**
Most homes are done in 3–5 hours. The Showstopper with roof wash typically runs 4–6 hours. We're in, we're done, we're out.

**Do I need to be home?**
No. We just need access to an exterior hose bib. Most of our customers aren't home. We'll send photos when we're done.

**How long will it last?**
Soft wash results typically last 1–3 years depending on your environment. Shaded, north-facing surfaces tend to re-grow algae faster. We recommend annual maintenance for most Northeast Ohio homes given the humidity.

**Do you do commercial properties?**
Yes. We work with property managers, HOAs, and commercial building owners across Cuyahoga, Lake, Lorain, Medina, Summit, Geauga, and Portage counties. Ask about volume pricing and quarterly agreements.

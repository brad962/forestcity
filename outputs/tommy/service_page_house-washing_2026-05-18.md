## The Problem

### Northeast Ohio Is Hard on Your Siding

Lake Erie humidity is relentless. We get more algae, more mold, more mildew than homeowners in drier climates — and it comes back faster. That green-and-gray creep on your siding isn't just dirt. It's a living organism that feeds on moisture and slowly degrades your exterior surfaces.

Road salt from Ohio winters makes it worse. Salt residue sits on lower siding panels all season, attracting grime and speeding up surface wear.

If you haven't had your house washed in two or three years, it's overdue. Most homeowners don't realize how bad it got until after we clean it.

## How We Do It Differently

### We Soft Wash. There's a Reason.

A pressure washer pointed at vinyl or aluminum siding can drive water behind the panels. Once moisture gets behind the siding, you're looking at rot, mold growth inside the wall, and a repair bill that's a lot bigger than a house wash.

We use **soft washing** — low-pressure water combined with a biodegradable cleaning solution. The solution kills the algae, mold, and mildew at the root. The rinse carries it away. Your siding is clean and undamaged.

The difference between soft washing and pressure washing isn't just technique. It's whether the algae actually dies or just gets knocked around to grow back in six weeks.

We do it right the first time.

## FAQ

**Q: Will soft washing damage my siding?**
No. Soft washing uses low pressure — lower than a garden hose on full blast. The cleaning solution does the work, not the water pressure. We've cleaned thousands of homes across Cuyahoga, Lake, Lorain, and the surrounding counties without a single siding damage claim.

**Q: How long does a house wash take?**
Most single-family homes take 2–4 hours. Larger homes or severe buildup may run longer. We'll give you a realistic time estimate when we book.

**Q: Do I need to be home?**
No. Most of our customers aren't home when we show up. We just need access to a water spigot. We'll send you a before/after photo when we're done.

**Q: How often should I have my house washed?**
In Northeast Ohio, once a year to once every two years is typical. The lake humidity means algae comes back faster here than in drier climates. If you're on the annual maintenance plan, we handle the timing for you.

**Q: What if I just want a price — no commitment?**
Call us. We'll give you a number over the phone for most standard homes. No pressure, no sales pitch. If you like the price, we book it. If not, no problem.

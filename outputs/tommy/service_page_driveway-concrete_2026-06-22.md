## THE PROBLEM

### It's Been Sitting There All Winter. And It Shows.

You drive past your driveway every single day, so you stop seeing it. Then one afternoon you walk to the end of the driveway, look back, and you're mortified. The oil stain by the garage. The gray-black tire tracks baked into the concrete. The salt lines from every cold snap since November.

That's the moment most of our customers call us.

Northeast Ohio is hard on concrete. Road salt gets tracked up every driveway from November through March — and then the freeze-thaw cycles grind the stains in deeper. By the time summer hits, you've got six months of buildup and it's not going anywhere without real pressure.

And if you've got company coming for the Fourth, a party on the deck, a home going on the market — suddenly it matters. A lot.

## HOW WE DO IT DIFFERENTLY

### Not Just a Garden Hose on Full Blast

A lot of guys rent a pressure washer and call it a job. That gets the loose stuff off the surface. It doesn't touch the oil, the deep salt staining, or the black tire marks that have baked into the pores of the concrete.

Here's what we do instead:

**1. We pre-treat first.**
Before we ever hit it with pressure, we apply a commercial-grade degreaser and concrete cleaner to the surface. That breaks down the oil and salt deposits at the root — not just the surface.

**2. We use a surface cleaner attachment, not just a wand.**
A surface cleaner spins at consistent pressure across the entire slab. No tiger-stripes, no uneven marks, no missed strips. Your driveway looks like a single clean surface when we're done — not a patchwork of lighter and darker spots.

**3. We rinse and edge.**
We don't leave cleaner sitting on your concrete. Full rinse, edges cleaned, downspout areas cleared out. We leave the job looking finished.

**What we don't do:** We don't pressure wash your roof, your wood siding, or your deck. Those surfaces need soft washing — and we do that too. But concrete? Concrete was made for pressure, and that's what we use.

## FAQ

**Q: Can't I just rent a pressure washer and do it myself?**

For basic rinsing, sure. But if you've got real oil stains or heavy salt buildup, a consumer-grade pressure washer and a standard wand won't cut it. You need the right pre-treatment, the right PSI, and a surface cleaner that covers the full slab evenly. Most people rent a washer, spend three hours on it, and end up with tiger-stripe marks they didn't have before. We've gotten calls from exactly those customers.

**Q: Will pressure washing damage my concrete?**

Not if it's done right. Concrete is designed for high-pressure cleaning. We adjust PSI based on the surface condition and age of the concrete. Newer stamped concrete or decorative surfaces get handled differently than a standard residential driveway. We'll tell you upfront if we see anything we're concerned about.

**Q: What about oil stains — will those actually come out?**

Older oil stains that have been there for years may lighten significantly but not disappear completely — we'll be honest with you about that. Fresh to medium-age stains come out very well with proper pre-treatment. Either way, your driveway will look dramatically cleaner than before we got there.

**Q: How long does it take?**

Most standard residential driveways take 45–90 minutes. If you're adding sidewalks, a patio, and steps, plan on 2–3 hours. We'll give you a time estimate when you book so you know what to expect.

**Q: Do I need to be home?**

Not unless there's a gate or access issue. We just need water access and clear parking. Most customers give us a heads-up about access and let us handle the rest. We'll text when we're on the way and when we're done.

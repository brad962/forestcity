*2026-05-23 — Use during and after every job*

## The Concept

When you're on a job, you're already the most credible sales tool in the neighborhood. Your truck is visible. The equipment is out. A freshly cleaned house is sitting right there as proof.

Three neighbors will notice. One will book.

The strategy: **10 minutes of knocking after every job = 1 extra booking per 3–4 jobs.** No ad spend. Zero travel overhead (you're already there). This is how small exterior cleaning companies double their revenue without doubling their marketing budget.

This playbook has four versions:
1. **Door knock (in-person)** — highest conversion
2. **Leave-behind card** — if nobody answers
3. **Same-street past customer text** — when you've worked nearby before
4. **"In your neighborhood" text** — for anyone in that zip from your list

---

## Version 1 — The Door Knock Script

**When to use:** Right after finishing a job. Knock on the 3–4 nearest neighbors.

**What to say (word for word):**

> "Hey — sorry to bother you. I'm Bradley with Forest City Power Washing. We just finished the house at [number] — you can see it from here actually. We're going to be in this neighborhood a few more times this month, and I wanted to see if you'd be interested in a quick quote. We're already here so there's no trip charge, and I can usually give you a same-week date."

**If they say "how much":**
> "Depends on the size and what you want done. For a typical house on this street with just siding, probably $250–$350. If you want a roof wash added, add another $150–$200. Takes a few hours, we're in and out. Want me to take a look right now while I'm here?"

**If they say "I'll think about it":**
> "Totally fine. Here's my card. If you text me this week I can get you on the schedule while we're still rotating through this area. Once we're gone it'll be a few weeks before we're back."
> *(Hand them a card. Walk away. Don't push.)*

**If they say "yes, take a look":**
> Walk the property. Look at siding, roof, driveway, deck. Give a verbal number. Say: "I can get you on the schedule for later this week if you want to lock it in now." Collect a deposit or at minimum a confirmed date via text.

**Conversion note:** The neighbor who watched you clean the other house is the warmest lead you'll ever get. A 30% close rate on door knocks after a visible job is realistic. Most contractors close 5–10% on cold knocks.

## Version 2 — The Leave-Behind Card

**When to use:** If nobody answers the door.

Print this as a half-sheet (2 per page). Black and white is fine. Handwrite the address of the house you just did — that's what makes it personal.

---

> **Hey neighbor —**
>
> We just finished the house at ___________. If your place has been on your list, this might be good timing — we're already in the neighborhood and can usually work in same-week visits when we're nearby.
>
> **Text or call Bradley: [phone]**
> forestcitypowerwashing.com
>
> *(We do siding, roofs, driveways, decks — full exterior or just one thing)*
>
> — Forest City Power Washing

---

**Handwrite the address.** "We just finished the house at 1847 Elm" hits differently than a generic flyer. It's not a coupon. It's a personal note.

## Version 3 — Same-Street Past Customer Text

**When to use:** When you're booked on a street or in a neighborhood where you've done work before.

**Look up** any past customers in Workiz within the same zip code or neighborhood. Text them the day before or morning of the job:

---

> **Text 1 (morning of the job):**
>
> "Hey [Name], it's Bradley from Forest City Power Washing. We're actually going to be right on your street tomorrow doing [neighbor's house]. Wanted to see if you needed anything cleaned while we're already out that way — always easier on the schedule (and usually a better price) when we're nearby. House wash, driveway, deck — whatever. Let me know. No pressure."

---

> **If they respond YES or "how much":**
>
> "Nice! What do you need — same full exterior as last time, or just one thing? I can give you a quick number. We'll be right there anyway so it's easy to add."

---

> **If no response (follow-up next day):**
>
> "Hey — just finished up on your street. Looks great over there. If you want to get on the schedule before summer gets fully booked, this week is actually a good time. Let me know."

---

**Why this works:** Past customers already trust you. Timing it when you're physically nearby lowers the activation energy to say yes. This is one of the lowest-cost ways to fill the schedule.

## Logistics

**How many doors to knock per job:** 3–5. The 2 directly adjacent and 2–3 houses across the street.

**When to knock:** DURING the job, not after. Knock before you start packing up. That way if a neighbor says "yes, look at mine," you can give a quote and possibly return the same afternoon or next day while equipment is still warm in your head.

**What to bring to every job:**
- 10 leave-behind cards (pre-printed — keep a stack in the truck)
- Your phone (for texting same-street prospects before/after)

**Timing for same-street texts:** Day before or morning of. "We're going to be on your street tomorrow" is the hook. "We're already there" is the close.

## Revenue Math

- Average job: $300
- Door knocks per job: 4
- Conversion rate: 1 in 4 (25% — realistic for warm neighbors watching the work)
- Extra job per job: 0.25 average
- 10 jobs/week × 0.25 = 2.5 extra jobs/week
- 2.5 × $300 = **$750/week in zero-cost neighbor revenue**
- Per month: **$3,000 in zero-ad-spend bookings**

That's more than Facebook ads will produce in week 1.

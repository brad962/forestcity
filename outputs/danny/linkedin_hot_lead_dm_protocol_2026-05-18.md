# LinkedIn Hot Lead Follow-Up Protocol
### Danny Malone | Outbound Sales | 2026-05-18
*For Bradley: use this with every contact who has 2+ email opens and no reply.*

---

## Who These Are

13 contacts opened our email sequence 2+ times and never replied. They're interested — they just didn't hit reply. LinkedIn is the right next move because:

1. It's a different channel (breaks the "email fatigue" pattern)
2. They can see your face, your company, your legitimacy
3. The connect request + note is softer than a cold call

---

## Step 1 — Send the Connection Request

Use ONE of these three notes (choose based on what you know about them):

---

### Note A — Direct and simple (best for property managers)

> Hi [First Name] — saw you manage properties in the [City] area. I run Forest City Power Washing and we work with a lot of PM companies across NE Ohio. Wanted to connect.

---

### Note B — Reference the email (if they opened 3+ times)

> Hi [First Name] — I sent you an email about exterior cleaning for your properties — looks like it landed. Figured LinkedIn was easier. Would love to connect.

---

### Note C — Social proof angle (best for realtors + HOA managers)

> Hi [First Name] — we've been working with a few property managers in [their county] on pre-listing and regular cleaning. Thought it'd be worth connecting in case timing ever lines up.

---

## Step 2 — They Accept: Send a Follow-Up Message (within 24 hours)

Don't pitch. Just open the door.

> Hey [First Name] — thanks for connecting. Quick question: do you handle exterior cleaning for your properties in-house or do you use a vendor?

**Wait for their answer before saying anything else.**

---

## Step 3A — They Answer "We Use a Vendor"

> Got it. Are you happy with them, or open to a second quote? We're usually 10–15% under market and we document everything with before/after photos — which property managers seem to appreciate for their records.

---

## Step 3B — They Answer "In-House"

> That makes sense for smaller stuff. Roof soft wash or large commercial washes are where most teams outsource — anything like that on your plate this season?

---

## Step 3C — They Don't Answer (2 days later)

> No pressure — just wanted to loop back. If the timing isn't right, I completely understand. We'll be in the area through the summer if anything comes up.

---

## Step 4 — They Express Interest: Book the Call or Walk-Through

> Perfect. Easiest thing is usually just a quick walk-through — takes 20 minutes, I'll give you a number on the spot. What's your schedule like this week or next?

---

## Tracking — Update Pipeline After Each Interaction

For each contact you reach out to, update their stage in the dashboard:
- Sent connect → "Contacted"
- Accepted + replied → "Replied"
- Call/walk-through booked → "Estimate Sent"

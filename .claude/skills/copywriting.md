# Skill: Copywriting

## Bradley's Voice — The Rules

Forest City copy sounds like Bradley: a Cleveland guy who knows his craft and takes pride in the work. Direct. Friendly. No agency fluff.

**Do:**
- Short sentences. Active voice.
- Contractions (we're, it's, you'll)
- "We" not "our team" or "our professionals"
- Specific > vague: "removes 10 years of algae stains" > "deep clean"
- Local references: Northeast Ohio, counties, neighborhoods
- Lead with the customer's problem, not our credentials

**Don't:**
- "World-class," "best-in-class," "industry-leading"
- "Leverage," "synergize," "solutions"
- "We are committed to providing excellent service" — show it, don't say it
- Passive voice: "Your home will be cleaned" → "We'll clean your home"
- Filler openers: "Are you tired of..." / "Do you dream of..."

## Headline Formulas (ranked by effectiveness for home services)

1. **Outcome + Location:** "Clean siding, clean roof, clean driveway — Northeast Ohio's exterior cleaning crew"
2. **Pain → Relief:** "Black streaks on your roof. We fix that."
3. **Social proof + specificity:** "500+ Northeast Ohio homes washed. No streaks, no damage."
4. **Direct offer:** "Get your house washed before the summer. Free quote in 24 hours."
5. **Question:** "When's the last time your siding was actually clean?"

## Body Copy Structure

**Problem → Agitate → Solve (PAS):**
1. Name the problem they already know they have
2. Make it feel real: what happens if it's ignored
3. Position Forest City as the relief

**For package descriptions:**
- Lead with the outcome the customer wants (not the list of services)
- State what's included in plain English
- Mention what makes it different from the tier above/below
- End with a simple CTA

## Writing for Different Formats

**Website headline (8 words or less):** Pure clarity. What you do, for who, where.
**Email subject line:** 5–7 words. Specific. No clickbait.
**Facebook post hook (first line):** Stop the scroll. Pain, curiosity, or bold claim.
**One-pager:** Lead with the problem, follow with solution, close with trust + CTA.
**Package description (50–80 words):** Outcome-first, contents, why it's popular, price signal, CTA.
**Estimate follow-up email:** Friendly, remind them what's included, light urgency, easy next step.

## Objection Handling Copy

When writing any sales copy, pre-empt the top 3 objections:
1. Price: Don't race to the bottom — explain the value difference
2. DIY: Acknowledge it's an option, explain the risk for specific surfaces
3. Timing: Light urgency without fake scarcity ("Spring books fast in Northeast Ohio")

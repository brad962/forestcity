# Skill: Sales Enablement

## Objection Handling — The Big 3

### Objection 1: "I can get it cheaper on Facebook."
**What they're really saying:** "I'm not sure you're worth the price difference."

Response framework:
1. Validate — "You can, and some of those guys do good work."
2. Reframe — "But a lot of them are one-man operations without insurance, and they don't always show up."
3. Differentiate — "We're not the cheapest and we don't pretend to be. We show up, we do it right, we're insured, and we stand behind the work."
4. Close — "If the guy on Facebook shows up and does great work, that's great. If he doesn't, we'll be here."

### Objection 2: "I'll just rent a pressure washer."
**What they're really saying:** "I'm not sure I need to pay someone for this."

Response framework:
1. Validate — "For a concrete driveway or patio, that totally works."
2. Educate — "The issue is if you're thinking about siding or your roof. A pressure washer drives water behind vinyl panels and can cause rot behind the walls. On a roof, it strips the granules off your shingles — that's the waterproofing layer — and can void your manufacturer warranty."
3. Reframe — "Soft washing uses low-pressure water and a cleaning solution that actually kills the algae. It's what roofing manufacturers recommend."
4. Close — "For driveways? Do it yourself. For anything on the house itself, soft wash is the right call."

### Objection 3: "I don't need it that often."
**What they're really saying:** "I'm not sure this is worth the recurring cost."

Response framework:
1. Acknowledge — "You don't need it every year — some homes are more sheltered and hold up better."
2. Add context — "In Northeast Ohio though, with the lake humidity, algae and mold come back in 12–18 months on most homes. Once you can see it, you've already got growth between the panels."
3. Risk reframe — "The bigger thing is the roof. Black algae (Gloeocapsa magma) actually feeds on the limestone filler in shingles. If it's left for years, it shortens the roof's lifespan."
4. Annual plan pitch — "Most of our customers do spring and fall. The annual plan gives them a discount and they don't have to think about it."

## Discovery Questions for Estimates

Before quoting, ask:
1. "How long have you been in the house?" (longer = more neglected, higher urgency)
2. "When's the last time it was cleaned?" (establishes baseline)
3. "Is there anything specific that's bothering you — the roof, the siding, the driveway?" (anchor to their pain)
4. "Are you thinking about listing it, or just keeping it maintained?" (pre-sale = highest urgency + budget)
5. "Have you had it done before?" (yes → familiar with value; no → may need more education)

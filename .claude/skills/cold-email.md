# Skill: Cold Email

## The Job
Write cold emails that get replies from property managers, facility managers, HOA contacts, and referral partners in Northeast Ohio.

## Core Framework: PASTA
- **P**ain — open with their specific problem
- **A**wareness — show you understand their world
- **S**olution — one sentence on what you do
- **T**rust — one proof point or credential
- **A**sk — one clear, low-friction CTA

## Subject Line Formulas
- Question: "Who handles exterior maintenance for [Company]?"
- Pain: "The algae on your building's siding — quick question"
- Local: "[City] property managers + exterior cleaning"
- Referral: "[Referral name] suggested I reach out"
- Direct: "Power washing for [Company's] properties"

Avoid: clickbait, fake "Re:" prefixes, overly clever wordplay

## Email Length
- Cold email: 4–6 sentences max. One screen on mobile.
- Never use bullet points in a cold email — reads like a brochure.
- One CTA only. Never give two options.

## CTAs That Work for This Business
- "Would a quick call this week make sense?" (most common)
- "Can I send over our service area and pricing?"
- "Do you have a vendor for exterior cleaning currently, or is that still open?"
- "Worth a 10-minute call to see if we're a fit?"

## Personalization by Prospect Type

**Property Managers:**
Angle: They manage multiple buildings. Reliable vendor relationships matter. They need someone who shows up.
Pain: Missed vendors, inconsistent quality, algae on buildings making the property look bad to tenants.
Hook: "Managing [X] properties in [County] — exterior cleaning is either a headache or it's handled."

**Facility Managers:**
Angle: They're responsible for appearance of commercial buildings. Accountability is key.
Pain: Algae on building siding, stained concrete, pressure from management to keep it looking professional.
Hook: "Noticed [building/company name]'s exterior — thought I'd reach out about a spring clean."

**HOA Managers:**
Angle: They manage community standards and homeowner expectations.
Pain: Getting complaints about algae on common area buildings, difficulty finding reliable vendors.
Hook: "A lot of HOAs in [County] have us handle the common area exteriors — wanted to see if [HOA name] is covered."

## Tone
- Sound like a real person, not a template
- No "I hope this email finds you well"
- No "I wanted to reach out because" — just reach out
- Cleveland-direct: short, confident, no wasted words

## Follow-Up Email Rules
- Follow up 3x before marking as dead
- Each email stands alone — don't say "following up on my last email"
- Change the angle each time: try a different pain point or add a proof point
- Day 1 → Day 4 → Day 8

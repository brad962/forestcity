# Skill: Social Content

## Platform Focus
- **Facebook** — primary. Homeowners 35–65. Local community feel.
- **LinkedIn** — secondary. Property managers, commercial prospects.

## Facebook Content Pillars

### 1. Before/After Posts (highest engagement)
Format:
- Hook: What the problem looked like ("This roof in Strongsville had 8 years of algae on it when we showed up.")
- Short middle: what we did / how long it took
- Reveal: "Here's what it looked like 2 hours later."
- Soft CTA: "DM us if yours looks like the before."
- Photo: before on left, after on right — or two photos side by side

Rules:
- Get specific — neighborhood, county, surface type
- Don't say "amazing results" — show them and let the reader react
- Never post without a photo

### 2. Educational Posts
Examples:
- "Why you should never pressure wash your roof (and what to do instead)"
- "What those black streaks actually are — and why they spread"
- "The difference between soft washing and pressure washing, in plain English"
- "Northeast Ohio homes and algae: why we have it worse than most"

Format: Quick explanation (3–5 short paragraphs), end with "Questions? Drop them in the comments."

### 3. Social Proof Posts
- Screenshot a 5-star Google review, add a comment about the job
- "We just wrapped up [job description] in [city]. Here's what [customer first name] had to say:"
- "50th job this spring — thank you, Northeast Ohio."

### 4. Seasonal/Local Posts
Connect the service to what's happening in Northeast Ohio right now:
- Spring: "Algae season is back. Here's what 6 months of Ohio winter did to this driveway in Medina."
- Fall: "Pre-winter clean is the move. Get the road salt and grime off before it freezes in."
- Summer: "Selling your home this summer? Curb appeal matters — here's what we can do in 3 hours."

### 5. Personality/Behind the Scenes
- Bradley on a job site
- Equipment/process ("This is how we soft wash a roof without risking the shingles")
- Team doing a big commercial job
- Local landmark or community connection

## Post Structure
1. **Hook** (first 1–2 lines — must work without "See more")
2. **Body** (2–4 short paragraphs)
3. **CTA** (soft — comment, DM, book, call)
4. **Hashtags** (3–5 only): #ClevelandOhio #NortheastOhio #PowerWashing #CurbAppeal #ForestCity

## What NOT to Post
- Stock photos of clean homes (needs to be real Forest City work)
- Generic "Happy Memorial Day!" with no connection to the business
- Anything that sounds like it came from a marketing template
- More than 5 hashtags (looks spammy)

## LinkedIn Post Format
- More direct / professional tone (still not corporate)
- Target: property managers, facility managers, commercial realtors
- Lead with a business problem
- 3–5 short paragraphs
- End with a direct question or observation
- No hashtag overload — 2–3 max

# Skill: Marketing Psychology

## Key Psychological Levers for Home Services

### 1. Social Proof
Neighbors trust neighbors. The most powerful proof for a home services company is:
- "500+ homes in Cuyahoga County cleaned"
- "4.9 stars on Google — 87 reviews"
- Neighborhood-specific: "We just finished a job two streets over in [neighborhood]"
- Before/after photos of real local homes

Application: Use in Facebook posts, email subjects, ad headlines, website hero section.

### 2. Loss Aversion (Fear of Damage)
Homeowners worry more about damage than they hope for improvement. Frame risks realistically:
- "Pressure washing a roof can strip the granules and void your warranty"
- "Algae left on siding for 3+ years eats into the surface"
- "One Ohio winter with road salt on your driveway = permanent etching"

Application: Educational content, objection handling for DIY objection, website FAQ.

### 3. Authority / Expertise
Soft washing knowledge differentiates Forest City from guy-with-a-pressure-washer competitors:
- Explain the soft wash process
- Reference manufacturer recommendations
- Show equipment and technique

Application: Website, one-pager, email value-adds.

### 4. Local Trust
People prefer to spend money with someone from their community:
- Reference specific Northeast Ohio cities and counties
- Mention the lake humidity and why it matters for algae growth
- Acknowledge Ohio winters and road salt
- Bradley's Cleveland background, Lutheran West graduate

Application: All copy — localize by county/city whenever possible.

### 5. Scarcity / Urgency (Real, Not Fake)
Spring and fall genuinely book up fast for exterior cleaning in Ohio. Use real urgency:
- "We book out 3–4 weeks in advance in April and May"
- "Fall clean window is only about 6 weeks before it gets too cold"
- Avoid fake countdown timers or manufactured "limited spots" — it reads as dishonest

Application: Seasonal promotions, end of email sequences.

### 6. Reciprocity
Give something before asking for something. Works for referral partner outreach and lead gen:
- Offer referral income before asking for referrals
- Lead magnets (Home Maintenance Checklist) give value before asking for email
- Send Marcus's competitor/VOC research to prospects as a "thought you'd find this interesting"

Application: Referral partner outreach, lead magnet strategy, cold email value-adds.

### 7. Commitment & Consistency
Once someone has taken a small step, they're more likely to take the next one:
- Annual plan customers who've done one cleaning are likely to rebook
- After a free quote, the "foot in the door" increases close rate
- Ask for micro-commitments: "Mind if I send over our pricing for your zip code?"

Application: Email sequence structure, annual plan upsell, CRM follow-up timing.

## VOC Integration
Always pull exact customer language from Marcus's VOC library before writing. Customers respond to seeing their own words reflected back at them:
- If they say "the siding looked green and slimy" → use "green and slimy" not "algae buildup"
- If they say "didn't know if they'd show up" → use "actually shows up" as a differentiator
- If they say "like a brand new house" → use that as an outcome phrase

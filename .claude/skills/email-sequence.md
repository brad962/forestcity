# Skill: Email Sequence

## What a Sequence Is
A series of 3 emails sent over 8–10 days to a cold prospect. Each email has a different angle. The goal is to get a reply — not to close the sale on email.

## 3-Touch Sequence Structure

### Email 1 — The Intro (Day 1)
**Goal:** Get on their radar. Make it clear why you're reaching out to them specifically.
- Personalized to their role/industry
- One pain point, one solution, one CTA
- Shortest of the three — 4 sentences max

### Email 2 — The Value Add (Day 4)
**Goal:** Give them something useful even if they don't reply.
- Lead with a relevant piece of info, stat, or local context
- Examples: "Northeast Ohio had one of the worst algae seasons in the last 5 years due to the wet spring" / "Soft washing extends the life of vinyl siding by preventing algae eating into the surface"
- Soft close: "Still happy to put together a quote if it's useful"

### Email 3 — The Break-Up (Day 8)
**Goal:** Permission to follow up later + final attempt.
- Short. Direct. No hard feelings.
- "I'll stop reaching out after this — but wanted to make sure this wasn't just bad timing."
- Leave the door open: "If exterior cleaning ever comes up, I'm the right call."
- Closes with name and direct number

## Sequence Variations by Segment

**Commercial / Property Managers — Reliability Angle:**
- Email 1: Who handles exterior maintenance? (pain: unreliable vendors)
- Email 2: What to look for in a power washing vendor (value content)
- Email 3: Breakup + "you'll be the first call when the current situation isn't working"

**Residential Homeowners — Curb Appeal Angle:**
- Email 1: Spring is here, here's what a year of Northeast Ohio weather did to your siding
- Email 2: Before/after story from similar neighborhood
- Email 3: Last chance for spring pricing, then we're booked into summer

**Referral Partners (Contractors/Realtors) — Mutual Value Angle:**
- Email 1: I refer my customers to [their trade] — thought there might be mutual value here
- Email 2: How the referral arrangement works (quick overview)
- Email 3: "Even if timing's off, keep my card for when a client asks about exterior cleaning"

## Formatting Rules
- Plain text emails only — no HTML templates for cold outreach
- No unsubscribe links in cold email (that's for broadcast email)
- Sign with first name + Forest City Power Washing + phone number
- Each email under 150 words

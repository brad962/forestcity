# Skill: Competitor Profiling

## Research Framework

For each competitor, build a complete profile. Data sources: Google Maps, their website, Facebook, Yelp, HomeAdvisor/Angi.

## Profile Template

```
Company: [Name]
Location: [City, Ohio]
Service Area: [Counties/cities]
Years in Business: [Estimate from website or Google]
Google Rating: [X.X stars] ([N] reviews)
Yelp Rating: [X.X stars] ([N] reviews)

SERVICES OFFERED:
- [List what they advertise]

PACKAGES / PRICING:
- [Any published pricing or price signals from reviews]

STRENGTHS (from positive reviews):
- [What customers love about them]

WEAKNESSES (from negative reviews):
- [What customers complain about]

WEBSITE QUALITY:
- [Good/Basic/Poor] — [notes on trust signals, photography, CTAs]

SOCIAL MEDIA:
- Facebook: [active/inactive, follower count if visible]
- Instagram: [same]

POSITIONING:
- [How do they describe themselves? Price-focused? Quality? Local?]

FOREST CITY OPPORTUNITY:
- [Specific gap or differentiator Forest City can exploit]
```

## Target Competitors to Profile (Northeast Ohio)

Start with companies that have 50+ Google reviews in the 7-county service area. Search:
- "power washing Cleveland Ohio"
- "house washing [county] Ohio"
- "soft wash Cleveland"
- "exterior cleaning northeast ohio"

## What to Look For

**Pricing gaps:** If competitors don't publish pricing, Forest City publishing it (even a range) builds trust.

**Reliability gaps:** "No-show" is the #1 complaint in home services. If a competitor has these reviews, Forest City's reliability message hits harder.

**Soft wash knowledge:** Many competitors claim to wash roofs but use pressure — Forest City's soft-wash expertise is a real differentiator for roofs and siding.

**Service area limits:** If a competitor doesn't serve all 7 counties, Forest City's full coverage is a USP.

**Response time:** Note if competitors are slow to respond on Google Messages or Facebook DMs — responsiveness is free differentiation.

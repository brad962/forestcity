# Skill: Lead Magnets

## What Makes a Good Home Services Lead Magnet
- Solves a real question the homeowner already has
- Takes 5 minutes to consume
- Makes them think "I should probably call someone about this"
- Collected via Kit.com landing page, delivered by email

## Priority Lead Magnets for Forest City

### 1. Northeast Ohio Home Exterior Maintenance Checklist ⭐ Build First
**Target:** Residential homeowners 35–65, Northeast Ohio
**Format:** PDF checklist, 1–2 pages
**Content:**
- Month-by-month exterior maintenance tasks specific to Northeast Ohio climate
- What to check in spring (post-winter damage, algae starting)
- Summer (peak algae growth, check for mold on north-facing surfaces)
- Fall (pre-winter prep, get the cleaning done before temps drop)
- Winter (ice dam prevention, inspect for damage)
- Each item notes when to DIY vs. call a professional

**Why it works:** Homeowners feel behind on maintenance. This validates the anxiety and positions Forest City as the expert.

**CTA in document:** "Questions about your exterior? Forest City serves [7 counties]. Free estimate at [phone/website]."

### 2. "Before You List" — Pre-Sale Exterior Guide
**Target:** Homeowners thinking about selling
**Format:** Short PDF, 1 page
**Content:**
- What buyers (and their agents) notice first
- Which exterior services add the most perceived value
- Realistic cost vs. value for exterior cleaning pre-sale
- Timeline: book at least 2 weeks before photography

**Why it works:** Pre-sale homeowners have urgency and budget. This captures them at peak intent.

### 3. "What Are Those Black Streaks?" Roof Guide
**Target:** Homeowners who've Googled "black streaks on roof"
**Format:** Short PDF or email sequence
**Content:**
- What causes black streaks (Gloeocapsa magma algae)
- Why it spreads (releases spores)
- Why pressure washing is dangerous on a roof
- What soft washing does and why manufacturers recommend it
- When to call vs. ignore it (damage timeline)

**Why it works:** High search intent. They already know they have a problem.

## Lead Magnet Delivery Setup (Kit.com)

1. Create a Kit.com form for each lead magnet
2. Embed on a simple landing page
3. Thank you email delivers the PDF
4. 3-email nurture sequence follows:
   - Email 1 (Day 0): Deliver the lead magnet + one sentence about Forest City
   - Email 2 (Day 3): One tip from the checklist + soft CTA
   - Email 3 (Day 7): "Ready for a quote?" — direct CTA with seasonal hook

## Nurture Sequence Framework

After lead magnet download:
1. **Day 0:** Deliver + "We're Forest City, serving Northeast Ohio. Call anytime: [phone]"
2. **Day 3:** One specific, useful tip related to the lead magnet topic
3. **Day 7:** "Spring cleaning season books up fast in Northeast Ohio — want us to do an estimate?"
4. **Day 21:** Seasonal follow-up if no action taken
5. **Ongoing:** Monthly email with seasonal content (Jasmine's Facebook posts repurposed)
